RFQ Tracker Integration

Status: proposed — documented, not yet built.

Summary

ENCON runs two related tools. The RFQ Enquiry Tracker logs every incoming request for quotation and follows it through to an offer. The Offer Generator (this tool) produces the priced offer itself. At the moment the two are not connected: the technical person generates the offer here and then uploads it and the costing into the tracker by hand. This page sets out how to join the two so that an offer is started from an enquiry (with the customer details already filled in) and its results return to the enquiry automatically — removing the manual upload and the re-keying.

The two systems

RFQ Enquiry Tracker. A shared web app the team uses to log and follow every enquiry. Each enquiry carries a reference (for example ENC/RFQ/2026/001), the company and contact, the item, who it is assigned to, the sales and technical owners, the target date, and the status. Once an offer exists, the enquiry also holds the offer number, offer date, the document and the costing. Every enquiry has its own Google Drive folder ("Costing & Offer") where the finished documents are kept.

Offer Generator. This tool. The estimator enters the process parameters for a piece of equipment; the tool sizes it, selects the components, prices the bill of materials, and produces the offer as Word and PDF together with a costing workbook, filing them to Google Drive.

Both run as separate web apps and both already work with Google Drive, which is what makes a clean, loose connection between them practical.

How it works today (manual)

  1. An enquiry is logged in the tracker with the company, contact and item.

  2. The technical person opens the Offer Generator separately and re-keys the customer and the item.

  3. The offer is generated — Word, PDF and the costing sheet.

  4. The technical person then uploads the offer and the costing into the tracker by hand, against the right enquiry, and types in the offer number and date.

This works, but it means the same information is entered twice, the upload is a manual step that can be missed or delayed, and the tracker is only as current as the last time someone remembered to update it.

Proposed integration

The two apps stay separate — each keeps its own database and its own deployment — and are joined loosely at two points: a link out from the tracker, and a small write-back interface into it. Nothing about either app’s internal working changes, which keeps the change low-risk and lets either app be updated on its own.

Reading the diagram

Green, solid = the proposed automatic flow.
Red, dashed = the manual hand-off the technical person does today.

The proposed flow has three steps:

  1. Start from the enquiry. On an enquiry in the tracker, a "Generate Offer" link opens the Offer Generator with that enquiry’s details already filled in — company, contact, mobile, email, the item, and the sales and technical owners — along with the RFQ reference.

  2. Produce the offer. The estimator completes the technical inputs and generates the priced offer as normal: the Word and PDF documents and the costing sheet.

  3. Write the result back. As the offer is finished, the generator sends the key results back to the tracker — the offer number, the offer date, the document link and the costing total — and the matching enquiry is updated automatically. The documents are filed into that enquiry’s existing "Costing & Offer" Drive folder. This replaces the manual upload and re-keying done today.

The RFQ reference (for example ENC/RFQ/2026/001) ties the two together. It travels out with the "Generate Offer" link and comes back with the write-back, so the generator always knows which enquiry an offer belongs to and the tracker always updates the right one. The offer number can also be matched on, for offers created the other way round.

Documents & Google Drive

Both apps already use Google Drive, and each enquiry already has its own "Costing & Offer" folder. In the integrated flow the generator files the finished offer and costing straight into that enquiry’s folder, and the document link it writes back points there — so the tracker, the enquiry folder and the offer all line up without anyone moving files by hand.

Rules & edge cases

  • Offer with no enquiry. The generator can still be used on its own (no RFQ reference); it simply skips the write-back. The integration is additive.

  • Re-issuing an offer. If an offer is regenerated for the same enquiry, the write-back updates the existing offer details rather than creating a second entry, and a new revision is filed to the same folder.

  • Offer number. If an enquiry does not yet have an offer number, the generator’s number becomes the enquiry’s offer number on write-back; if it already has one, that number is reused.

  • Access. The write-back is a controlled interface between the two apps, so only the generator can update enquiry offer details — the rest of the tracker is unaffected.

What changes for each role

  • Technical person / estimator — no more re-keying the customer, and no more manually uploading the offer and costing into the tracker.

  • Sales / reviewer — the tracker shows the latest offer number, date, document and costing against each enquiry as soon as the offer is produced.

Build outline (when approved)

On the tracker side: add a "Generate Offer" link on the enquiry that opens the generator with the enquiry details, and a small write-back interface that accepts the offer results and updates the enquiry.

On the generator side: accept the pre-filled details when opened from an enquiry, carry the RFQ reference through, and call the tracker’s write-back once the offer is produced (and file the documents to the enquiry’s Drive folder).

Status & next steps

This integration is documented but not yet built. The agreed approach is to keep both apps separate and connect them through a link and a small interface, as above. The next step is to approve the build and schedule the two small pieces of work on each side.
