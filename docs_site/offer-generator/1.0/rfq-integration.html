RFQ Tracker Integration

Status: proposed — documented, not yet built.

Why integrate

ENCON runs two related tools. The RFQ Enquiry Tracker logs every incoming request for quotation and follows it through to an offer — it records the company, the contact, the item, who it is assigned to, the target date, and, once an offer exists, the offer number, offer date, document and costing. The Offer Generator (this tool) is what actually produces that priced offer.

Today the two are used side by side. The team logs an enquiry in the tracker, then separately opens the generator, re-keys the customer and the item, and produces the offer. The technical person then manually uploads the finished offer and the costing into the RFQ tracker against that enquiry, and types in the offer number and date. Connecting the two removes that manual upload and re-keying, and keeps the tracker up to date on its own.

How they connect

The two apps stay separate — each keeps its own database and its own deployment — and are joined loosely through a web link and a small interface. Neither app’s internal workings change; they simply hand information to each other at two points. This keeps the integration low-risk and lets either app be updated on its own.

The flow

  1. Start from the enquiry. On an enquiry in the tracker, a "Generate Offer" link opens the Offer Generator with that enquiry’s details already filled in — company, contact, mobile, email, the item, and the sales and technical owners — along with the RFQ reference.

  2. Produce the offer. The estimator completes the technical inputs and generates the priced offer as normal: the Word and PDF documents and the costing sheet, filed to the shared drive.

  3. Write the result back. As the offer is finished, the generator sends the key results back to the tracker — the offer number, the offer date, the document link and the costing total — and the matching enquiry is updated automatically. This replaces the step the technical person does by hand today, uploading the offer and costing into the tracker; the offer can also be filed straight into that enquiry’s existing "Costing & Offer" drive folder.

What is shared

Sent to the generator (pre-fill) Returned to the enquiry (write-back)

Company, contact, mobile, email

Offer number

Item / equipment description

Offer date

Sales & technical owners

Document link (Word / PDF)

RFQ reference

Costing total

The RFQ reference ties the two together: the generator always knows which enquiry an offer belongs to, and the tracker always shows the latest offer against each enquiry.

Status & next steps

This integration is documented but not yet built. The agreed approach is to keep both apps separate and connect them through a link and a small interface. Building it would mean adding the "Generate Offer" link on the tracker, having the generator accept the pre-filled details, and a small write-back step once an offer is produced.
