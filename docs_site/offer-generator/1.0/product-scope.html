Product Scope & Roadmap

Last updated: 2026-06-10

Overview

The Offer Generator is an internal tool that produces priced commercial offers for ENCON’s heating equipment. An estimator picks the product, enters the process details — fuel, capacity, temperatures, control type and so on — and the tool sizes the equipment, selects the right components, prices the full bill of materials, and produces a ready-to-send Word and PDF offer along with a costing sheet. Before this, every offer was put together by hand in spreadsheets and Word: a few hours each, and easy to get wrong as prices moved.

This page is the scope and roadmap. The target is ENCON’s full product range — the lines on the company’s Products menu — and for each one it records whether the tool produces that offer today, produces part of it, or doesn’t cover it yet. It is meant to be the single answer to "what can the generator quote, and what is still to come".

Offer-generation flow

Every product follows the same path. The estimator opens the form for that equipment and enters the process inputs. The tool sizes the equipment (pipe sizes, burner rating, blower duty, recuperator surface), selects the matching components against the catalogue and the current price list, builds the priced bill of materials grouped by system, applies the standard margin, and arrives at the total. From that it produces the formatted offer document and PDF, and a costing workbook; the finished offer can be filed straight to the shared drive.

Detailed system flow — all paths

The larger diagram adds the rest. The Combined Offer path lets several pieces of equipment be quoted together as one document, under one customer and one set of terms and conditions. It also shows how the live price list and the component catalogue feed the pricing, and how a finished offer goes out — as Word and PDF, as a costing Excel, and up to the shared drive. The price-list side is included too: a new vendor price list is loaded as an update and the rates flow through to every offer.

Product coverage map

The map sets every line in the catalogue against its build status, so the whole picture can be read at a glance. Green is live, blue is part-set-up only, amber is partial, and grey is not started.

The diagrams are maintained as source files under diagrams/ and regenerated with a single command, so they can be updated as the roadmap moves.

Coverage details

Iron & Steel Making

This is the most complete part of the tool and the core of ENCON’s preheating business. The vertical, horizontal and tundish preheaters are fully built and in daily use; they are closely related products, and most of the recent work — sizing accuracy, scope of supply, technical specifications, combined offers — has gone into them. The rest of the family is not yet covered: torpedo preheaters, the impeller dryer, the launder/runner/chutes dryer, the ladle and tundish cooler, the refractory dry-out burner, the RAYCON patch-up lining heater, the scrap dryer, sub-merged nozzle (SEN) heating and vessel preheating. The torpedo preheater at least has a sample offer on file to base the content on.

Combustion Equipment

These are the burners, blowers, recuperators and pumping units that also go into the larger preheater packages but can be quoted on their own. Blowers, burners, recuperators, and both the heating-and-pumping unit and the pumping-only unit are done. Regenerative burners are only part-set-up, not really started. Fuel and air skids are not a separate offer yet — they are currently built only as part of the larger packages. Self-recuperative burners, specialised burners, die/bolster heating and the hot air generator are not started.

Furnaces · Ovens · Other

The least developed area. The billet reheating furnace and the box-type furnace are part-set-up but not really started. Rad-heat gas elements have only pricing entered, with no offer form on top yet. Ovens, the other specialised fabrications and the testing ovens and furnaces have not been started.

Cross-cutting capabilities

Three capabilities run across all the products. The Combined Offer brings several pieces of equipment into one document under a single customer and one set of terms. Price management keeps the vendor rates current — a new price list is loaded as an update, so any manual rate changes already made are kept rather than overwritten. And finished offers are filed automatically to the shared drive for sharing and record.

Summary

29 product lines in total: 8 are fully built and in use, 3 are part-set-up only, 2 are partially covered, and 16 are not yet started. In short, the core ladle and tundish preheating business and the common combustion components are automated; the furnaces, dryers, coolers and specialised heaters are the bulk of what is still to build.
