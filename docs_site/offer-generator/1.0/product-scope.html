Product Scope & Roadmap

Last updated: 2026-06-10

Overview

The ENCON Offer Generator is an internal web application that turns equipment parameters into complete, priced commercial offers. An estimator selects a product, enters a handful of process and configuration parameters, and the system performs the engineering sizing, selects the correct catalogue components, builds a fully-priced Bill of Materials, and produces a formatted Word and PDF offer together with a multi-sheet costing workbook — work that previously took hours of manual spreadsheet effort for every enquiry.

This document defines the target scope — ENCON’s full product catalogue, as published on the company’s Products menu — and records, for each product line, whether it is already automated, partially built, or still pending. It is the single reference for what the tool covers today and what remains on the roadmap.

Offer-generation flow

Every supported product follows the same pipeline. The estimator opens the relevant equipment form and supplies the process inputs — fuel, capacity, temperatures, control mode and so on. The application calculates the engineering sizing (gas and combustion-air pipe sizes, burner firing rate, blower duty, recuperator surface), and the selection engine then picks the matching catalogue parts: burner, gas train, air-gas ratio control, blower, air duct, valves, rotary joint and pumping unit. From these it assembles a priced Bill of Materials, grouped by system, applies the standard markup, and arrives at a cost summary. Finally the offer is rendered into the Word template, converted to PDF and — optionally — uploaded to Google Drive, while a parallel route produces the multi-sheet costing Excel.

Coverage details

Iron & Steel Making

The iron-and-steel range is the heart of ENCON’s preheating business and the most heavily automated part of the tool. The three ladle and tundish preheaters — vertical, horizontal and tundish — are fully built and in daily use. The remainder of the family (torpedo preheaters, the various dryers and coolers, refractory dry-out and patch-up lining heaters, sub-merged nozzle and vessel preheating) is still to be added; the torpedo preheater already has a reference offer to work from.

Combustion Equipment

Combustion equipment covers the burners, blowers, recuperators and pumping units that also appear as components inside the larger preheater offers. Blowers, burners, recuperators, and both the heating-and-pumping and pumping-only units are complete and can be offered stand-alone. Regenerative burners are scaffolded only; fuel-and-air skids exist today purely as sub-assemblies inside other offers; and the remaining specialised burners and heaters are pending.

Furnaces · Ovens · Other

The furnace, oven and specialised-fabrication lines are the least developed. Two reheating furnaces — billet (SNSF BRF) and box-type — have scaffolding in place but no real implementation; rad-heat gas elements exist only as price data with no user interface; and the remaining ovens and testing furnaces are not yet started.

Cross-cutting capabilities

Beyond the individual product lines, three capabilities support every offer. The Combined Offer builder merges several pieces of equipment into a single document under one customer and one set of terms and conditions. Pricelist and price-master management keeps the underlying vendor rates current, applying new price lists as deltas so manual rate edits are preserved. And finished offers are uploaded automatically to Google Drive for sharing and record.

Summary

Of the twenty-nine distinct catalogue lines, eight are complete and in use, three exist only as scaffolding, two are partially covered, and sixteen are still to be built. In short, the core ladle-and-tundish preheating business and the common combustion components are automated, while the broader furnace, dryer, cooler and specialised-heating families make up the main body of work that remains.
