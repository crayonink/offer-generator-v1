Vertical Ladle Preheater (VLPH)

A vertical ladle preheater dries and heats the refractory lining of a steel ladle before molten metal is poured into it. A burner fires downward into the ladle from a hood that sits on top, so the ladle is heated while standing upright. This page explains, in plain terms, what the user fills in, what the finished offer contains, and how the price is arrived at.

What you enter

The heating job. Either describe the process and let the system size the burner, or enter the burner size directly.

  • Process method: the start and finish temperature of the lining, the weight of refractory to be heated, how long the heating should take, the system efficiency, and the ladle capacity (in tons).

  • Direct method: simply type in the burner capacity or the heat input, and the system uses that instead of the process maths.

The fuel. The main fuel (natural gas, LPG, coke-oven gas, blast-furnace gas, mixed gas, or an oil) and its calorific value. You can add a second fuel for a dual-fuel unit, and choose the pilot burner.

The control system. Whether the unit is operated manually or automatically. Automatic units can be PLC- or PID-based, which changes the instruments included.

The makes. Which supplier to use for the control valve, shut-off valve, butterfly valve and pressure gauge; and, for oil fuels, the type of oil pumping unit (Simplex / Duplex).

The air and pipework. The blower pressure rating, and the weight of the air–gas pipeline (this figure is always entered by hand).

Other options. The hood type (up-down or swivel), whether a nitrogen purging line is needed, whether special ignition equipment is wanted, and optional overrides for the structure weight or ceramic-fibre quantity.

Quantities and commercials. How many vertical (and horizontal) sets the offer covers, and the commercial figures: the mark-up on bought-out items, the packaging & forwarding percentage, the designing percentage, the negotiation percentage, and a flat transport amount.

What the offer contains

A priced bill of materials, grouped by section — the combustion-air line, the fuel line, the pilot line, the ENCON-built equipment (burner, blower, structure, pipeline, ceramic fibre, and for oil the pumping unit), and the instruments and control panel. Each line shows the item, quantity, make, unit price and total.

The offer document (a Word file, plus a PDF), containing:

  • a Scope of Supply that lists, in words, everything ENCON will provide;

  • a Price Schedule with the all-inclusive price, and transport shown on its own separate line;

  • the Terms & Conditions (prices, delivery, taxes, payment, validity, guarantee), which can be edited before generating;

  • a Make List naming the supplier for each major item.

The document is saved, converted to PDF, and a copy is filed automatically.

How the price is worked out

  1. Work out the fuel and air needed. From the heating figures (or the direct capacity you typed), the system calculates how much fuel per hour and how much combustion air the burner needs, adding a small safety margin.

  2. Choose the equipment. Using those flows it sizes the gas and air pipework, then picks the right burner, gas train, air–gas regulator (where used), blower and valves from the catalogue — each chosen to comfortably handle the required flow.

  3. Size the air line from the blower. The combustion-air line is sized from the airflow of the blower that was actually selected, so the pipe, the valve and the displayed air flow all agree.

  4. Fill in the structure and ceramic. The structure weight and ceramic-fibre quantity are filled in automatically from the ladle size (you can override them).

  5. Price every line and total it up. Each item is priced from the rate list. Bought-out items are summed and multiplied by the mark-up; ENCON-made items are added on top, giving the grand total.

  6. Add the commercials. Packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation are each added as a percentage of the grand total (they add to the price, they do not discount it), the flat transport is added, and the result is rounded to the nearest ₹1,000.

  7. Produce the documents. The final all-inclusive price goes into the offer; transport is then shown on its own line so the schedule still adds up. The Word file and PDF are created and filed.
