Recuperators

Module: recup. Form: /recup → recup_costing.html. Status: ✅ Complete. A heat-transfer-sized product: the air-preheater surface area is computed from the flue/air duty, then the tube grid and BOM are derived.

Inputs

The Recuperator form lives at /recup (main.py:2588). Step 1/2 inputs map to RecupCalcRequest (main.py:2595) → RecupInputs (calculations/recup.py:42); Step 3 commercial inputs and Step 4 offer metadata map to RecupQuoteRequest (main.py:2698).

Offer / T&C metadata in RecupQuoteRequest (main.py:2698): customer block (salutation, poc_name, company_name, company_address, email, mobile_no, project_name, subject, application, client_enq_ref), marketing/technical contacts, location (default "FBD"), supervision lines (supervision_mech, supervision_plc), price_schedule_style ("single" or "full"), optional full-mode supervision row, and the tnc_* fields.

Outputs

/api/recup-calculate (main.py:2619) returns a JSON object with three blocks; /api/generate-recup-quote (main.py:2830) renders the offer document.

Sizing results (calculations block, from RecupResults):

  • Heat balance: flue_mass_kghr (E4), heat_required_kcal (E13), flue_temp_out_C (derived, E7), lmtd_C (E14, with 0.9 factor).

  • surface_area_m2 — required heat-transfer area (E15).

  • Pipe grid: pipes_total_raw (E19), pipes_total, pipes_in_row, pipes_in_column, pipes_per_bank.

  • Dimensions: bank_length_mm (E16), bank_width_mm (E17).

  • Weights: weight_per_pipe_kg, weight_hot_bank_kg, weight_cold_bank_kg, weight_total_pipes_kg, and the MS structural weights ms_outer_shell_kg, ms_air_inlet_duct_kg, ms_hot_outlet_duct_kg, ms_pipe_holding_kg, ms_bottom_box_kg.

  • Echoed process inputs for the offer’s Designing Parameters table.

BOM / price schedule (bom block, built by build_recup_df, bom/recup_builder.py:67): one row per line item with MEDIA, ITEM NAME, REFERENCE, QTY, MAKE, UNIT PRICE, TOTAL. Line items:

  • BOUGHT OUT / MISC: Thermocouple with TT (flat).

  • ENCON ITEMS: SS304/MS ERW Tube — Hot Bank, SS304/MS ERW Tube — Cold Bank, MS Side Hood (2 Nos) Fabrication, MS Combustion Air Inlet Assembly, the stock structural steel rows (MS Channel 150×75×10, MS Angle 65×25, 75×10, 50×15), Pipe Bending, Welding Rods, Hole Fabrication.

  • Summary rows: BOUGHT OUT ITEMS, ENCON ITEMS, GRAND TOTAL.

Cost summary (cost_summary block, recup_summary, bom/recup_builder.py:217): bought_out_total, encon_total, grand_total, markup_conversion, sale_after_markup, designing_pct, negotiation_pct, final_total (rounded up to nearest Rs 1000).

Offer document (/api/generate-recup-quote):

  • Scope-of-supply / Material of Construction table built by _material_of_construction (main.py:2751): SS→"SS-316", MS→"Mild Steel (CS)"; hot bank rendered as bent tubes, cold bank as straight; tube spec carries OD + Schedule-40.

  • Price Schedule rows assembled by _bom_rows_for_offer (main.py:2775); in "single" style one row at the final price + amount in words, in "full" style every BOM line plus optional supervision row and bought-out/ENCON/grand totals.

  • Designing Parameters table (echoed flue/air conditions, surface area, pipe geometry), plus a marketing-rounded air-outlet temperature (ceil to next 50 °C).

  • Optional 3D recuperator images (side/front/top) embedded from static/recup/*.jpeg when present (main.py:2948).

  • Output template: Recup_Offer_Template.docx (repo root, main.py:2939), cloned from the VLPH Offer_Template.docx via build_recup_template_from_vlph.py. Saved as Recup_Offer_<company>_<seq>.docx in QUOTES_FOLDER, with best-effort PDF conversion and async Google-Drive mirroring (product_type "recuperator").

Calculation Flow

  1. Flue mass (E4). flue_mass = flue_flow_nm3hr × 1.2 (calculations/recup.py:168).

  2. Heat duty (E13). Air mass = air_volume_nm3hr × 1.2; heat_required = air_mass × cp_air × (air_temp_out − air_temp_in) (calculations/recup.py:171–173).

  3. Final flue temp (E7). Back-solved from the heat balance: flue_temp_out = flue_temp_in − heat_required / (flue_mass × cp_flue) (calculations/recup.py:176).

  4. LMTD (E14). dT1 = flue_temp_in − air_temp_out, dT2 = flue_temp_out − air_temp_in; lmtd = dT1 − dT2)/ln(dT1/dT2 × 0.9 (degenerate cases fall back to the arithmetic mean) (calculations/recup.py:179–185).

  5. Surface area (E15). surface_area = heat_required / (lmtd × heat_transfer_coef) (calculations/recup.py:188).

  6. Raw pipe count (E19). pipes_raw = surface_area / (3.14 × (dia/1000) × (length + 0.1)) — literal 3.14 to match the spreadsheet (calculations/recup.py:193–195).

  7. Grid selection (E20/E21). Target = pipes_total_override if set, else ceil(pipes_raw). _best_grid (calculations/recup.py:121) picks a near-square (rows, cols) — cols forced even so each bank gets a whole number — scoring aspect_pct + 3×waste_pct, tie-breaking toward rows=14. pipes_total = rows × cols, pipes_per_bank = pipes_total // 2 (calculations/recup.py:200–205).

  8. Bank dimensions (E16/E17). bank_length_mm and bank_width_mm derived from the grid, pipe pitch (32 mm), diameter, and 150 mm end margin (calculations/recup.py:208–213).

  9. Pipe weights (E27/E28/E35). weight_per_pipe = pipe_kg_per_m × length; hot and cold banks each = weight_per_pipe × pipes_per_bank (calculations/recup.py:216–219).

  10. MS structural weights (E41–E45). Outer shell, air-inlet duct, hot-outlet duct, pipe-holding plate, and bottom box computed by volume × density (_vol_to_kg), auto-scaling with bank geometry (calculations/recup.py:222–238).

  11. BOM assembly + pricing. /api/recup-calculate loads recup_rates via _load_rates (bom/recup_builder.py:57) and calls build_recup_df (bom/recup_builder.py:67). Rates resolved from DB with Excel-default fallbacks: tube rates SS304_TUBE_PER_KG (250) / MS_TUBE_PER_KG (70) per bank material; MS_PER_KG (60) for stock steel; MS_FABRICATION_PER_KG (70) for the CAI assembly. Rows priced:

    1. Tubes: hot/cold = weight_bank_kg × tube_rate (bom/recup_builder.py:117–130).

    2. MS Combustion Air Inlet Assembly (F49): (shell + inlet + outlet + holding + box + flanges_kg(100) + side_hood_kg) × cai_rate, where cai_rate = cai_rate_override if >0 else MS_FABRICATION_PER_KG (bom/recup_builder.py:145–166).

    3. MS Side Hood fabrication (F48): flat SIDE_HOOD_COST (50000) (bom/recup_builder.py:135).

    4. Stock steel (F50–53): length_m × kg_per_m × MS_PER_KG, specs from _STOCK_STEEL_SPECS (bom/recup_builder.py:170–180).

    5. Labour: Pipe Bending (rows+cols) × 350, Welding Rods n_total × 4 × 8, Hole Fabrication n_total × 2 × 100 (bom/recup_builder.py:183–200).

    6. Thermocouple: flat THERMOCOUPLE_TT (8000) (bom/recup_builder.py:112). Summary rows sum BOUGHT OUT and ENCON to GRAND TOTAL (bom/recup_builder.py:206–214).

  12. Commercial markup. recup_summary (bom/recup_builder.py:217) applies the Excel additive chain: sale = grand × MARKUP_CONVERSION (1.8); final = sale × (1 + designing + negotiation), rounded up to the next Rs 1000 (bom/recup_builder.py:229–242). The Step-3 UI (recup_costing.html:957–968) reproduces and extends this client-side — final = sale + sale×pf + sale×design + sale×nego + transport, rounded up to Rs 1000 — and posts the result as final_total (+ transport_amt on its own line).

  13. Offer context build. /api/generate-recup-quote (main.py:2830) computes unit_price = final_total, total_price = unit_price × qty, generates the enquiry ref/date, and assembles the docxtpl context: Designing Parameters, Material of Construction (main.py:2751), and the price schedule rows (main.py:2775) per price_schedule_style.

  14. Docx render & delivery. Loads Recup_Offer_Template.docx (main.py:2939), embeds the optional 3D images (main.py:2948), renders, saves to QUOTES_FOLDER as Recup_Offer_<company>_<seq>.docx, attempts PDF conversion, mirrors to Google Drive async (product_type "recuperator"), and returns download / PDF / preview URLs (main.py:2956–2994).

The backend recup_summary markup is the additive Excel chain (conversion → designing + negotiation only). The actual offer’s final_total is computed client-side in recup_costing.html and additionally includes Packaging & Forwarding (%) and a flat Transport amount, then rounds up to the nearest Rs 1000. P&F and Transport are UI-only commercial inputs that never pass through the Python calc — they arrive at the backend already baked into final_total (with Transport also echoed separately as transport_amt).
