Heating & Pumping Unit (HPU)

A heating and pumping unit takes fuel oil, heats it so it flows and atomises properly, and pumps it to the burner at the right pressure. It is the oil-handling skid between the oil tank and the burner. (For oils that are already hot enough and only need pumping, see the Pumping Unit.)

What you enter

What you enter Typical value / options

Variant

Simplex (single), Duplex 1 (default), or Duplex 2 (with standby)

Capacity

9 kW (choices: 3, 6, 9, 12, 16, 20, 24, 30, 36, 48)

Oil flow rate

filled in automatically from the capacity and variant — you don’t type it

Quantity

1 (number of units)

Packaging & forwarding

5 %

Designing

10 %

Negotiation

0 %

Transport

flat ₹ amount (default 0)

Customer details and Terms & Conditions

company, contacts, subject, and the standard terms

There is no fuel drop-down or combustion setting here — this is purely the oil heating-and-pumping skid.

What the offer contains

The offer document (Word + PDF) includes:

  • A scope section headed "Oil Heating and Pumping Unit" describing a heating-and-pumping skid of the chosen variant, with bullets listing:

    • the pump(s), each with its electric motor (two for Duplex, one for Simplex);

    • an electric oil pre-heater with thermostatic control (two for Duplex 2, otherwise one);

    • duplex coarse and fine filters (cold and hot side);

    • a pressure-regulating valve;

    • a pressure gauge and a temperature gauge.

  • A price schedule with a single all-inclusive line. Transport, if entered, appears on its own separate line. Packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation are built into the unit price, not shown as separate lines.

  • The grand total in figures and words.

  • The Terms & Conditions.

A running total updates live as you change the inputs, and the finished offer can be previewed before sending.

How the price is worked out

  1. Look up the oil flow rate for the chosen capacity and variant.

  2. Build the unit price by adding up the catalogue costs of the heating-and-pumping unit’s parts and applying the standard mark-up.

  3. Multiply by the quantity.

  4. Add the commercials — packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation are each added as a percentage (they add to the price, they do not discount it), and the flat transport is added.

  5. Round the total to the nearest ₹1,000 when any commercial figure has been applied.

  6. Produce the documents — the all-inclusive price goes into the offer, transport is shown on its own line, and the Word file and PDF are created and filed.

Worked example

Suppose the unit’s parts (pumps, motors, heater, filters, valves, gauges) add up to a catalogue cost of ₹1,00,000.

  • Unit price = 1,00,000 × 1.8 (standard mark-up) = ₹1,80,000

  • Quantity 1, P&F 5 % + designing 10 % + negotiation 0 % (= +15 %): 1,80,000 × 1.15 = 2,07,000 → rounded to the nearest ₹1,000 = ₹2,07,000

Transport, if any, is added to that total and then shown on its own line in the price schedule.
