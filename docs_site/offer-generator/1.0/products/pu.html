Pumping Unit (PU-only)

A pumping unit pumps fuel oil to the burner at the right pressure. It is the same skid as the Heating & Pumping Unit but without the heater — used for oils that are already hot enough (or heated elsewhere) and only need pumping.

What you enter

What you enter Typical value / options

Variant

Simplex (single), Duplex 1 (default), or Duplex 2 (with standby)

Capacity

9 kW (choices: 3 … 48)

Oil flow rate

filled in automatically from the capacity and variant

Quantity

1 (number of units)

Packaging & forwarding

5 %

Designing

10 %

Negotiation

0 %

Transport

flat ₹ amount (default 0)

Customer details and Terms & Conditions

company, contacts, subject, and the standard terms

There are no heating, combustion or burner settings — the pumping unit is just the oil-pumping skid.

What the offer contains

The offer document (Word + PDF) includes:

  • A scope section headed "Pumping Unit" describing a pumping skid of the chosen variant, with bullets listing:

    • the pump(s), each with its electric motor;

    • duplex coarse and fine filters (cold and hot side);

    • a pressure-regulating valve;

    • a pressure gauge.

      Compared with the Heating & Pumping Unit, there is no oil pre-heater and no temperature gauge — that is the main difference between the two products.

  • A price schedule with a single all-inclusive line; transport on its own line; packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation built into the unit price.

  • The grand total in figures and words.

  • The Terms & Conditions.

How the price is worked out

  1. Look up the oil flow rate for the chosen capacity and variant.

  2. Take the unit price straight from the catalogue. (Unlike the Heating & Pumping Unit, the price is read directly — it already allows for the mark-up, and there is no heater to add.)

  3. Multiply by the quantity.

  4. Add the commercials — packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation added as percentages (they add to the price, they do not discount it), plus flat transport.

  5. Round the total to the nearest ₹1,000 when any commercial figure has been applied.

  6. Produce the documents — the all-inclusive price goes into the offer, transport is shown on its own line, and the Word file and PDF are created and filed.

Worked example — default unit

Chosen unit = 9 kW, Duplex 1 (model PUD-9, oil flow 120 LPH). Its catalogue price (already mark-up-included, no heater) = ₹1,13,798.

  • Quantity 1, P&F 5 % + designing 10 % + negotiation 0 % (= +15 %): 1,13,798 × 1.15 = 1,30,867 → rounded to the nearest ₹1,000 = ₹1,31,000

Compare the Heating & Pumping Unit — the same 9 kW Duplex 1 unit there is ₹1,19,326 (final ₹1,37,000) because it adds an oil pre-heater and temperature gauge. The pumping unit is ₹5,528 cheaper at the unit-price level for every size.

Full calculation reference (every step)

A pumping unit offer is a catalogue pick — the same pump skid as the Heating & Pumping Unit but with the oil pre-heater and temperature gauge removed. This section documents every step with the real catalogue figures.

The three variants

Variant What’s in it

Simplex (model PUS-)

One pump + motor — no standby

Duplex 1 (model PUD-, default)

Two pumps + motors (one running, one standby)

Duplex 2 (model PUDD-)

Two pumps + motors with extra redundancy

Every variant has duplex coarse and fine filters, a pressure-regulating valve and a pressure gauge. None has a heater or temperature gauge — that is the whole difference from the heating-and-pumping unit.
