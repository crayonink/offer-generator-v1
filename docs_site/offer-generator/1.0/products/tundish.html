Tundish Preheating System

A tundish preheating system heats the refractory lining of a tundish (the holding vessel that feeds molten steel into a continuous caster) before it goes into service. It works just like the vertical ladle preheater, with one important difference: a tundish is usually heated by several burners at once rather than a single burner. This page explains the inputs, the output, and how the price is built, and points out the few tundish-specific extras.

What you enter

The same details as the vertical ladle preheater — the heating job, the fuel, the control system, the makes, the air and pipework, and the commercial figures — plus three tundish-specific items:

  • Number of burners — how many burners share the heating. The total firing rate is split between them to size each burner, and the burner-related items in the bill of materials are multiplied by this number.

  • Structure-weight override — type in the MS structure weight to use for the fabrication cost, instead of the figure the system would look up.

  • Ceramic-fibre override — type in the number of ceramic-fibre rolls, instead of the looked-up figure.

What the offer contains

The same priced bill of materials and offer document (Word + PDF) as the ladle preheaters:

  • the burner (and its pilot and ignition parts) counted once per burner;

  • the shared equipment — blower, gas train, valves, structure, pipeline, ceramic fibre — counted once;

  • a Scope of Supply, a Price Schedule with transport on its own line, and editable Terms & Conditions;

  • the equipment is named "Tundish Preheater" in the document.

The tundish offer is produced from the same standard offer document as the other preheaters — there is no separate tundish template.

How the price is worked out

  1. Work out the total fuel and air needed from the heating figures or the direct capacity.

  2. Split the firing rate across the burners. The shared equipment (blower, gas train, main line) is sized for the total flow, but each burner is sized for its own share.

  3. Choose the equipment — pipework, burner, gas train, regulator, blower and valves from the catalogue.

  4. Size the air line from the chosen blower’s airflow.

  5. Fill in the structure and ceramic figures from the ladle/tundish size, or use your overrides.

  6. Price every line and total it up, multiplying the per-burner items by the number of burners.

  7. Add the commercials — packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation as percentages, plus flat transport, rounded to the nearest ₹1,000.

  8. Produce the documents — the all-inclusive price in the offer, transport on its own line, saved as Word and PDF.
