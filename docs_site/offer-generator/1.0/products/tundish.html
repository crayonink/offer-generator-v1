Tundish Preheating System

A tundish preheating system heats the refractory lining of a tundish (the vessel that feeds molten steel into a continuous caster) before it goes into service. It works just like the vertical ladle preheater, with one important difference: a tundish is usually heated by several burners at once.

What you enter

The same details as the vertical ladle preheater:

What you enter Typical value / options

Sizing method

"Calculate from process" (default) or "Enter burner size directly"

Lining start / finish temperature

650 °C / 1200 °C

Refractory weight

21,500 kg

Heating time

2 hours

Efficiency

0.52

Ladle / tundish capacity

10 tons

(Direct) Burner capacity

kW, taken as the size per burner

(Direct) Heat input

kcal/hr (overrides capacity)

Main fuel + calorific value

Natural Gas (default) / LPG / COG / BFG / Mixed Gas / RLNG / oils; 8,500 kcal/Nm³

Second fuel + its calorific value

None (default), or any of the above for dual-fuel

Control mode / automatic type

Automatic (default) or Manual; PLC / PLC+AGR / PID

Makes

control valve, shut-off valve, butterfly valve, pressure gauge

Blower pressure

28 (default) or 40 inches water gauge

Air–gas pipeline weight

1,000 kg

Oil pumping unit type (oil fuels)

Simplex / Duplex 1 (default) / Duplex 2

Purging line

No (default) or Yes

Plus three tundish-specific items:

What you enter Typical value / options

Number of burners

1 — the total firing rate is split between them, and the burner-related items are multiplied by this number

Structure-weight override

kg (leave 0 to fill in automatically)

Ceramic-fibre override

number of rolls (leave 0 to fill in automatically)

And the commercial figures — bought-out mark-up (1.8), packaging & forwarding (5 %), designing (5 %), negotiation (0 %), and a flat transport amount.

What the offer contains

The same priced bill of materials and offer document (Word + PDF) as the ladle preheaters:

  • The burner and its pilot and ignition parts — counted once per burner.

  • The shared equipment — blower, gas train, air-gas regulator / control valve, butterfly and shut-off valves, gate valve, pressure gauges and switches, rotary joint, structure, air–gas pipeline and ceramic fibre — counted once.

  • The oil pumping unit for oil fuels.

  • Totals — bought-out, ENCON and grand total.

The offer document carries the Scope of Supply (the equipment is named "Tundish Preheater"), the Price Schedule with transport on its own line, the editable Terms & Conditions, and the Make List. It is produced from the same standard offer document as the other preheaters.

How the price is worked out

  1. Work out the total fuel and air needed from the heating figures or the direct capacity.

  2. Split the firing rate across the burners — the shared equipment is sized for the total flow, but each burner is sized for its own share.

  3. Choose the equipment — pipework, burner, gas train, regulator, blower and valves from the catalogue.

  4. Size the air line from the chosen blower’s airflow.

  5. Fill in the structure and ceramic figures from the size, or use your overrides.

  6. Price every line and total it up, multiplying the per-burner items by the number of burners.

  7. Add the commercials — packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation as percentages, plus flat transport, rounded to the nearest ₹1,000.

  8. Produce the documents — the all-inclusive price in the offer, transport on its own line, saved as Word and PDF.
