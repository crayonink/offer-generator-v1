Blowers

A blower supplies combustion air to a burner. This offer sells a blower on its own (with its motor and base frame), separate from a full preheater. It is a "pick from the catalogue" offer — you choose a model and the system prices it.

What you enter

What you enter Typical value / options

Customer details

company, address, contacts, and the subject of the offer

Blower model

chosen from a drop-down of catalogue blowers — each model already carries its horsepower, airflow and pressure rating, so nothing is sized by hand

Quantity

1 (number of blower sets)

Packaging & forwarding

5 %

Designing

10 %

Negotiation

0 %

Transport

flat ₹ amount (default 0)

The introduction paragraph and the bullet list of what’s supplied are written automatically from the model you pick — you don’t type them.

What the offer contains

The offer document (Word + PDF) includes:

  • A scope paragraph describing the blower — a centrifugal steel-plate air blower of the chosen horsepower and airflow, with a suitable electric motor on a common base frame.

  • A bulleted list of what’s supplied:

    • the centrifugal steel-plate air blower (its horsepower and airflow);

    • a suitable electric drive motor;

    • a common base frame with anti-vibration pads.

  • A price schedule with the quantity, unit price and total. If transport was entered, it appears on its own separate line.

  • The grand total in figures and words.

  • The Terms & Conditions.

While filling in the form, a running total updates live as you change the quantity or any commercial figure, and the finished offer can be previewed before sending.

How the price is worked out

  1. Look up the chosen blower in the catalogue and take its standard price (the price including the motor).

  2. Multiply by the quantity.

  3. Add the commercials — packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation are each added as a percentage (they add to the price, they do not discount it), and the flat transport is added.

  4. Round the total to the nearest ₹1,000 when any commercial figure has been applied.

  5. Produce the documents — the price goes into the offer, transport is shown on its own line, and the Word file and PDF are created and filed.

This standalone offer simply supplies the blower, motor and base frame — it does not size any air pipework. The air-line sizing rule (sized from the blower’s airflow) only applies inside the full preheater offers.
Worked example — standalone offer

Chosen blower = ENCON 28/15 (15 HP, 2,890 Nm³/hr); catalogue price with motor = ₹1,48,000; quantity 1; P&F 5 % + designing 10 % + negotiation 0 % (= +15 %).

  • 1,48,000 × 1 × 1.15 = 1,70,200 → rounded to the nearest ₹1,000 = ₹1,70,000

For 2 blowers: 1,48,000 × 2 × 1.15 = 3,40,400 → ₹3,40,000. If transport were ₹4,000, it is added to the total and then shown on its own line in the price schedule.

Full calculation reference (every step)

A standalone blower offer is a catalogue pick — no sizing is done. But the same blower catalogue and the selection rule are what the preheater offers use internally, so both are documented here.

How a blower is selected (inside the preheater offers)

# Step Worked (default VLPH job)

1

Combustion air needed (from the burner calc)

2,180.23 Nm³/hr

2

CFM = air ÷ 1.7

2,180.23 ÷ 1.7 = 1,282.49 CFM

3

Required HP = CFM × pressure(″WG) ÷ 3,200

1,282.49 × 28 ÷ 3,200 = 11.22 HP

4

Pick the smallest model in the chosen pressure series with HP ≥ required

28″ series, ≥ 11.22 HP → ENCON 28/15 (15 HP, 2,890 Nm³/hr, 1,700 CFM)

5

Air-line NB = selected blower CFM × 1.7, sized at 15 m/s

1,700 × 1.7 = 2,890 Nm³/hr → 300 NB

So the chosen blower is always one size up from the bare requirement (here 15 HP for an 11.22 HP need), and the air pipe is sized from the blower it will actually feed — not from the raw combustion-air figure — so the displayed flow and the pipe agree. For dual-fuel units the blower is sized for the larger of the two fuels' air demands; for oil, atomising-air CFM = equivalent LPH × 10.

The 1.7 is just the unit conversion between CFM (cubic feet per minute — how blowers are rated) and Nm³/hr (how the combustion calc gives the air figure): 1 CFM ≈ 1.7 Nm³/hr. So required air ÷ 1.7 gives CFM to pick the blower, and the chosen blower’s rated CFM × 1.7 gives the air-line flow to size the pipe. The full round-trip is documented in the vertical-preheater reference.
