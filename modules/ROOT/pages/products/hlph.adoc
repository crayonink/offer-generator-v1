= Horizontal Ladle Preheater (HLPH)
:description: What a Horizontal Ladle Preheater offer needs, what it produces, and how its price is worked out — in plain language.

A horizontal ladle preheater does the same job as the xref:products/vlph.adoc[vertical preheater] — drying and heating a ladle's refractory lining — but the ladle is heated lying on its side. The burner fires in from the side, and the ladle sits on a powered trolley instead of under a swivel hood.

TIP: As with the vertical unit, a hot ladle (start temperature *above 600 °C*) switches to *Circulating Ladle Preheater* mode and sizes the burner for *half* the refractory weight. See xref:products/vlph.adoc#_circulating_ladle_preheater[Circulating Ladle Preheater].

NOTE: There is no hood choice here — a horizontal unit uses a trolley, not a swivel or up-down hood.

== What the offer contains

The same kind of priced bill of materials and offer document (Word + PDF) as the vertical unit, with these horizontal-specific points:

* *A trolley drive group* (in place of the vertical unit's swivel/hood parts) — geared motor mechanism, four trolley wheels, plummer blocks, one long and two short shafts, trolley bearings, sleeves, and a structural frame.
* *No rotary joint* — the horizontal unit has no rotating hood.
* *Combustion-air line* — compensator, pressure gauge with isolation valve, low-pressure switch; on PLC, the orifice plate and flow transmitter; control valve and butterfly valve.
* *Fuel line(s)* — gas train, air-gas regulator, shut-off / ball / solenoid valves, regulator and hoses; second fuel line for dual-fuel.
* *Optional* — nitrogen purging line; and, when special ignition is on, the pilot line plus ignition transformer, sequence controller, UV sensor and pilot burner.
* *ENCON-built items* — fabricated structure, air–gas pipeline, ceramic fibre, the burner, the blower, and (for oil fuels) the heating & pumping unit.
* *Instruments and panel* — control panel, instrument ball valves, and PLC-with-HMI on PLC modes.
* *Totals* — bought-out, ENCON and grand total.

The offer document carries the Scope of Supply, the Price Schedule (transport on its own line), the editable Terms & Conditions and the Make List.

== How the price is worked out

. *Work out the fuel and air needed* from the heating figures or the direct capacity.
. *Choose the equipment* — pipework, burner, gas train, regulator, blower and valves sized to the required flows.
. *Size the air line from the chosen blower's airflow* so the pipe and the displayed flow agree.
. *Fill in the structure, ceramic and trolley-drive figures* from the ladle size (overridable).
. *Price every line and total it up* — bought-out items times the mark-up, plus ENCON-made items.
. *Add the commercials* — packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation as percentages, plus flat transport, rounded to the nearest ₹1,000.
. *Produce the documents* — the all-inclusive price in the offer, transport on its own line, saved as Word and PDF.

There is also a *cost-comparison tool* that re-runs the calculation across single changes — swapping a vendor, turning ignition on or off, changing the pumping-unit type, or flipping between manual and automatic — so the effect of each choice on the price can be seen side by side.

[NOTE]
.Worked example — start to finish (every number)
====
*Given inputs (defaults):* lining 650 → 1200 °C, refractory 21,500 kg, heat factor 0.25, efficiency 0.52, heating time 2 hr, natural gas at 8,500 kcal/Nm³, blower pressure 28″ WG, 10-ton ladle, automatic (PLC + AGR) control, single burner.

*Step 0 — Circulating check.* Ti = 650 °C is *above 600 °C*, so this is a *Circulating Ladle Preheater*: the hot, in-circulation ladle only needs topping up, so the effective weight is half the input = 21,500 ÷ 2 = *10,750 kg*. Every figure below uses it. (Same rule as the vertical unit — see xref:products/vlph.adoc#_circulating_ladle_preheater[Circulating Ladle Preheater].)

*Step 1 — Fuel & air needed* (identical to the vertical unit — same burner math):

* Average temperature to raise = (1200 − 650) ÷ 2 + 50 = *325 °C*
* Firing rate = *10,750* × 0.25 × 325 = *8,73,437.5 kcal* (uses the effective weight)
* Heat load = 8,73,437.5 ÷ 0.52 = *16,79,687.5 kcal*
* Fuel for the 2-hr cycle = 16,79,687.5 ÷ 8,500 = 197.61 Nm³ → ÷ 2 hr = 98.81 Nm³/hr → +10 % margin = *108.69 Nm³/hr* (gas flow)
* Firing rate in MW = 108.69 × 8,500 ÷ (860 × 1000) = *1.07 MW*
* Combustion air = 8,500 × 108.69 × 118 ÷ 1,00,000 = *1,090.12 Nm³/hr*
* Blower air = 1,090.12 ÷ 1.7 = *641.25 CFM*; reference HP at 28″ = 641.25 × 28 ÷ 3,200 = *5.61 HP*

*Step 2 — Equipment selected from the catalogue:*

* Burner = *ENCON 5A*; Blower = *ENCON 28/7.5* (7.5 HP, 1,450 Nm³/hr, 850 CFM); Gas train = *DN40 × DN40*

*Step 3 — Pipe sizing:*

* Gas line: 108.69 Nm³/hr at 12.7 m/s → *65 NB*
* Air line: selected blower 850 CFM × 1.7 = 1,445 Nm³/hr at 15 m/s → *200 NB*

*Step 4 — Structure, ceramic & trolley drive.* Auto-filled from the 10-ton *horizontal* ladle mapping (MS structure kg, ceramic-fibre rolls); these feed the per-kg ENCON lines (fabrication ₹110/kg, pipeline ₹125/kg) and are *not* affected by the circulating halving. The trolley-drive group (geared motor, wheels, plummer blocks, shafts, bearings, frame) replaces the vertical unit's swivel hood and rotary joint.

*Step 5 — BOM totals* (every line priced from the pricelist + selectors):

* Bought-out items total = *₹6,54,018.25*
* ENCON-made items total = *₹6,82,319.21*

*Step 6 — Commercials* (mark-up 1.8, P&F 5 %, designing 5 %, negotiation 0 %):

* Grand Total = bought-out × 1.8 + ENCON = 6,54,018.25 × 1.8 + 6,82,319.21 = 11,77,232.85 + 6,82,319.21 = *₹18,59,552*
* + P&F 5 % + Designing 5 % (= +10 %) + Negotiation 0 % + Transport ₹0 = 20,45,507
* Round to nearest ₹1,000 = *₹20,46,000* (the single all-inclusive price; transport, if any, on its own line)

The figures are *lower than the vertical unit* (≈ ₹25,94,000 for the same circulating job) because the horizontal BOM has no rotary joint and a lighter ENCON structure.

== Full calculation reference (every step)

The Horizontal Ladle Preheater uses *exactly the same sizing math* as the xref:products/vlph.adoc[vertical preheater] (`calculations/burner.py`, `calculations/pipes.py`, `bom/selectors/selection_engine.py`) — *including the Circulating Ladle Preheater rule*: when Ti > 600 °C the effective refractory weight is halved before sizing (the worked example above is circulating). The only differences are in the bill of materials (`bom/hlph_builder.py`): a *trolley drive* instead of a swivel hood, and *no rotary joint*. This section documents every formula, with the worked values from the example above.

=== A. Sizing

==== Average temperature to raise

The number that drives the burner size is picked by *two branches* depending on how hot the ladle is at the start (`Ti`):

[cols="2,3,3",options="header"]
|===
| Ladle state | Formula | Why
| *Warm start* — Ti > 100 °C (ladle already in cycle) | (Tf − Ti) / 2 + 50 | Take *half the temperature span* — heating ramps up rather than jumping straight to Tf — plus a *+50 °C* offset. A reheated ladle needs less energy than its absolute temperatures suggest, so this delta-based figure is more realistic than a plain mean.
| *Cold start* — Ti ≤ 100 °C (effectively from ambient) | (Tf + Ti) / 2 | A plain mean of start and finish temperatures.
|===

* *Warm-start example (defaults):* Ti 650, Tf 1200 → (1200 − 650) / 2 + 50 = 275 + 50 = *325 °C*
* *Cold-start example:* Ti 30, Tf 1200 → (1200 + 30) / 2 = *615 °C*

The `Ti > 100` threshold is the switch between "warm / in-cycle ladle" and "cold start". The warm-start branch deliberately yields a *smaller* rise (and thus a smaller burner) than a naive mean would.

[cols="1,4,2",options="header"]
|===
| # | Formula | Worked (defaults — circulating, Ti 650)

| 0 | *Effective weight* = entered weight ÷ 2 if Ti > 600 (circulating), else entered weight | 21,500 ÷ 2 = *10,750 kg*
| 1 | *Average temp rise* — warm start (Ti > 100): (Tf − Ti)/2 + 50; cold start (Ti ≤ 100): (Tf + Ti)/2 | (1200−650)/2 + 50 = *325 °C*
| 2 | *Firing rate* (kcal) = effective weight × heat factor × avg-temp-rise | 10,750 × 0.25 × 325 = *8,73,437.5*
| 3 | *Heat load* = firing rate ÷ efficiency | ÷ 0.52 = *16,79,687.5 kcal*
| 4 | *Fuel consumption* = heat load ÷ CV | ÷ 8,500 = *197.61 Nm³*
| 5 | *Calculated firing rate* = fuel ÷ time | ÷ 2 = *98.81 Nm³/hr*
| 6 | *+10 % margin* → gas flow | × 1.1 = *108.69 Nm³/hr*
| 7 | *Firing rate (MW)* = flow × CV ÷ (860 × 1000) | = *1.07 MW*
| 8 | *Combustion air* = CV × flow × 118 ÷ 1,00,000 | = *1,090.12 Nm³/hr*
| 9 | *CFM* = air ÷ 1.7; *blower HP* = CFM × pressure ÷ 3,200 | 641.25 CFM; *5.61 HP*
|===

*Circulating Ladle Preheater* — when Ti > 600 °C the effective weight in step 0 is halved (the hot ladle only needs topping up); only the burner/blower/pipe sizing uses it, not the structure/ceramic quantities. Identical rule to the xref:products/vlph.adoc#_circulating_ladle_preheater[vertical unit].

*Direct mode* (instead of steps 0–6): heat = burner kW × 860 (or the entered heat input); gas flow = heat ÷ CV; air = heat × 118 ÷ 1,00,000.

*Pipe sizing* (`pipes.py`) — area = (flow ÷ 3600) ÷ velocity; inner dia = √(4·area/π) × 1000, rounded up to the next standard NB:

* Gas line at 12.7 m/s → 65 NB; air line uses the *selected blower's* CFM × 1.7 (1,445 Nm³/hr) at 15 m/s → 200 NB. Oil-fuel line NB: ≤ 250 LPH → 20 NB, else 25 NB.

*Equipment selection* (`selection_engine.py`) — same as the vertical unit:

* *Burner* — smallest ENCON size whose max firing covers the gas flow at the burner pressure (ENCON 5A).
* *Blower* — required HP = CFM × pressure ÷ 3,200; pick the smallest blower in the chosen pressure series at or above it (ENCON 28/7.5). Dual-fuel sizes for the larger air demand.
* *Gas train* — the row whose [min, max] flow band brackets the gas flow (DN40 × DN40).
* *Air-gas regulator* — only for Manual / PLC+AGR / PID; sized from the gas-train outlet NB.
* *Butterfly & valves* — sized to the air-line NB.

For the full air → CFM → blower → air-line round-trip and why the *1.7* factor appears twice, see xref:products/vlph.adoc#_combustion_air_cfm_and_the_air_line[Combustion air, CFM and the air line] in the vertical-preheater reference — the logic is identical.

=== B. Costing (`bom/hlph_builder.py`)

Each `_row` is priced by a computed override or an exact pricelist lookup:

* *Fabrication / structure* = MS structure kg × ₹110/kg
* *Air-gas pipeline* = pipeline kg × ₹125/kg
* *Ceramic fibre* = roll count × ceramic rate
* *Trolley drive* (horizontal-specific, replaces the swivel hood + rotary joint) = geared motor, four wheels, plummer blocks, one long + two short shafts, bearings, sleeves and frame — each an exact pricelist lookup
* *Burner, blower, gas train, AGR, valves, HPU* = the prices returned by the selectors / pricelist
* *Bought-out instruments & panel* (compensator, pressure gauge, switch, orifice, flow transmitter, control valve, PLC, control panel, etc.) = exact pricelist lookups
* Rows are split into *bought-out* (everything not tagged "ENCON ITEMS") and *ENCON* items.
