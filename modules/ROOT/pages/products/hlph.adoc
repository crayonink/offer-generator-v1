= Horizontal Ladle Preheater (HLPH)
:description: What a Horizontal Ladle Preheater offer needs, what it produces, and how its price is worked out — in plain language.

A horizontal ladle preheater does the same job as the xref:products/vlph.adoc[vertical preheater] — drying and heating a ladle's refractory lining before metal is poured — but the ladle is heated lying on its side. The burner fires in from the side, and the ladle sits on a powered trolley instead of under a swivel hood. Most of the offer works exactly like the vertical unit; this page focuses on the differences.

== What you enter

The same set of details as the vertical preheater:

* *The heating job* — start and finish temperature, refractory weight, heating time, efficiency and ladle size; or the burner capacity entered directly.
* *The fuel* — main fuel and calorific value, an optional second fuel, and the pilot burner.
* *The control system* — manual or automatic (PLC / PID variants).
* *The makes* — control valve, shut-off valve, butterfly valve, pressure gauge, and the oil pumping-unit type for oil fuels.
* *The air and pipework* — blower pressure rating, and the air–gas pipeline weight (always entered by hand for the horizontal unit).
* *Options* — purging line, special ignition, and overrides for structure weight or ceramic quantity.
* *Commercials* — mark-up, packaging & forwarding, designing, negotiation, and transport.

The one thing that does *not* apply here is the hood choice — a horizontal unit uses a trolley, not a swivel or up-down hood.

== What the offer contains

The same kind of priced bill of materials and offer document (Word + PDF) as the vertical unit, with these horizontal-specific points:

* *A trolley drive group* — geared motor, trolley wheels, plummer blocks, shafts, bearings, sleeves and a structural frame — in place of the vertical unit's swivel/hood parts.
* *No rotary joint* — the horizontal unit has no rotating hood, so that part is left out.
* Everything else — combustion-air line, fuel line, burner, blower, instruments, scope of supply, price schedule (with transport on its own line) and terms & conditions — follows the same pattern as the vertical preheater.

== How the price is worked out

The steps are the same as the vertical preheater:

. *Work out the fuel and air needed* from the heating figures or the direct capacity.
. *Choose the equipment* — pipework, burner, gas train, regulator, blower and valves sized to the required flows.
. *Size the air line from the chosen blower's airflow* so the pipe and the displayed flow agree.
. *Fill in the structure, ceramic and trolley-drive figures* from the ladle size (overridable).
. *Price every line and total it up* — bought-out items times the mark-up, plus ENCON-made items.
. *Add the commercials* — packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation added as percentages, plus flat transport, rounded to the nearest ₹1,000.
. *Produce the documents* — the all-inclusive price in the offer, transport on its own line, saved as Word and PDF.

There is also a *cost-comparison tool* that re-runs the calculation across single changes — swapping a vendor, turning ignition on or off, changing the pumping-unit type, or flipping between manual and automatic — so the effect of each choice on the price can be seen side by side.
