= Blowers
:description: What a standalone Blower offer needs, what it produces, and how its price is worked out — in plain language.

A blower supplies combustion air to a burner. This offer sells a blower on its own (with its motor and base frame), separate from a full preheater. It is a "pick from the catalogue" offer — you choose a model and the system prices it.

== What you enter

[cols="3,4"]
|===
|What you enter |Typical value / options

|Customer details |company, address, contacts, and the subject of the offer
|Blower model |chosen from a drop-down of catalogue blowers — each model already carries its horsepower, airflow and pressure rating, so nothing is sized by hand
|Quantity |1 (number of blower sets)
|Packaging & forwarding |5 %
|Designing |10 %
|Negotiation |0 %
|Transport |flat ₹ amount (default 0)
|===

The introduction paragraph and the bullet list of what's supplied are written automatically from the model you pick — you don't type them.

== What the offer contains

The offer document (Word + PDF) includes:

* *A scope paragraph* describing the blower — a centrifugal steel-plate air blower of the chosen horsepower and airflow, with a suitable electric motor on a common base frame.
* *A bulleted list* of what's supplied:
** the centrifugal steel-plate air blower (its horsepower and airflow);
** a suitable electric drive motor;
** a common base frame with anti-vibration pads.
* *A price schedule* with the quantity, unit price and total. If transport was entered, it appears on its own separate line.
* *The grand total* in figures and words.
* *The Terms & Conditions.*

While filling in the form, a running total updates live as you change the quantity or any commercial figure, and the finished offer can be previewed before sending.

== How the price is worked out

. *Look up the chosen blower* in the catalogue and take its standard price (the price including the motor).
. *Multiply by the quantity.*
. *Add the commercials* — packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation are each added as a percentage (they add to the price, they do not discount it), and the flat transport is added.
. *Round* the total to the nearest ₹1,000 when any commercial figure has been applied.
. *Produce the documents* — the price goes into the offer, transport is shown on its own line, and the Word file and PDF are created and filed.

NOTE: This standalone offer simply supplies the blower, motor and base frame — it does not size any air pipework. The air-line sizing rule (sized from the blower's airflow) only applies inside the full preheater offers.

[NOTE]
.Worked example — standalone offer
====
Chosen blower = *ENCON 28/15* (15 HP, 2,890 Nm³/hr); catalogue price *with motor* = ₹1,48,000; quantity 1; P&F 5 % + designing 10 % + negotiation 0 % (= +15 %).

* 1,48,000 × 1 × 1.15 = 1,70,200 → rounded to the nearest ₹1,000 = *₹1,70,000*

For 2 blowers: 1,48,000 × 2 × 1.15 = 3,40,400 → *₹3,40,000*. If transport were ₹4,000, it is added to the total and then shown on its own line in the price schedule.
====

== Full calculation reference (every step)

A standalone blower offer is a *catalogue pick* — no sizing is done. But the same blower catalogue and the *selection rule* are what the preheater offers use internally, so both are documented here.

=== The blower catalogue (`blower_master`)

Every model carries its own horsepower, airflow, CFM, pressure rating and two prices (*basic* = blower only; *premium* = with motor — the standalone offer always uses the premium "with-motor" price). Models are named `ENCON <pressure series>/<HP>`, e.g. *ENCON 28/15* = 28″ W.G. series, 15 HP.

A parallel *40″ W.G.* series (ENCON 40/5 … 40/60) carries the same HP steps at lower airflow for the same HP (higher pressure costs flow) and slightly higher prices. The 40″ series is chosen when blower pressure is set to 40 instead of 28.

=== How a blower is selected (inside the preheater offers)

[cols="1,4,2",options="header"]
|===
| # | Step | Worked (default VLPH job)
| 1 | *Combustion air* needed (from the burner calc) | 2,180.23 Nm³/hr
| 2 | *CFM* = air ÷ 1.7 | 2,180.23 ÷ 1.7 = *1,282.49 CFM*
| 3 | *Required HP* = CFM × pressure(″WG) ÷ 3,200 | 1,282.49 × 28 ÷ 3,200 = *11.22 HP*
| 4 | *Pick* the smallest model in the chosen pressure series with HP ≥ required | 28″ series, ≥ 11.22 HP → *ENCON 28/15* (15 HP, 2,890 Nm³/hr, 1,700 CFM)
| 5 | *Air-line NB* = selected blower CFM × 1.7, sized at 15 m/s | 1,700 × 1.7 = 2,890 Nm³/hr → *300 NB*
|===

So the chosen blower is always *one size up* from the bare requirement (here 15 HP for an 11.22 HP need), and the air pipe is sized from the blower it will actually feed — not from the raw combustion-air figure — so the displayed flow and the pipe agree. For dual-fuel units the blower is sized for the *larger* of the two fuels' air demands; for oil, atomising-air CFM = equivalent LPH × 10.

=== Pricing (standalone offer)

[cols="3,3,2",options="header"]
|===
| Step | Formula | Worked
| Unit price | catalogue *with-motor* price | ₹1,48,000 (ENCON 28/15)
| Line total | unit × quantity | × 1 = ₹1,48,000
| Commercials | × (1 + P&F% + Designing% + Negotiation%) | × 1.15 = ₹1,70,200
| Final | round to nearest ₹1,000 (when any commercial applied) | *₹1,70,000*
|===

NOTE: This standalone offer simply supplies the blower, motor and base frame — it does *not* size any air pipework. The selection rule and air-line sizing above only run inside the full preheater offers. P&F, designing and negotiation are all *added* (negotiation does not discount); transport is added to the total and then shown on its own line.
