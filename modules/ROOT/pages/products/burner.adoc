= Burners
:description: What a standalone Burner offer needs, what it produces, and how its price is worked out — in plain language.

A burner mixes fuel and air and provides the flame that heats a furnace or ladle. This offer sells a burner on its own, separate from a full preheater. Like the blower offer, it is a "pick from the catalogue" offer — you choose a pre-priced burner and the system prices it.

== What you enter

[cols="3,4"]
|===
|What you enter |Typical value / options

|Burner type |ENCON gas burner (default), ENCON oil burner, ENCON dual-fuel burner, or a GAIL pre-assembled gas set
|Burner model |the specific size, chosen from a drop-down for the selected type
|Quantity |1 (number of burners)
|Packaging & forwarding |5 %
|Designing |10 %
|Negotiation |0 %
|Transport |flat ₹ amount (default 0)
|Customer details and Terms & Conditions |company, contacts, subject, and the standard terms
|===

The fuel, the firing capacity and the unit price are filled in automatically from the catalogue once you pick a model — you don't type them. (Firing capacity is shown in litres per hour for ENCON sizes, or in kilowatts for GAIL sets.)

== What the offer contains

The offer document (Word + PDF) includes:

* *A scope paragraph* — "we will supply N burner(s) of capacity … to achieve the desired temperature", followed by the main components.
* *A bulleted list* of the burner package:
** the burner;
** a ball valve;
** a CI burner mounting plate;
** a burner block;
** a set of flexible hoses;
** a butterfly valve.
* *A price schedule* with quantity, unit price and total; transport, if entered, on its own line.
* *The grand total* in figures and words.
* *The Terms & Conditions.*

A running total updates live as you change the quantity or commercial figures, and the finished offer can be previewed before sending.

== How the price is worked out

. *Look up the chosen burner* in the catalogue and take its price.
. *Multiply by the quantity.*
. *Add the commercials* — packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation are each added as a percentage (they add to the price, they do not discount it), and the flat transport is added.
. *Round* the total to the nearest ₹1,000 when any commercial figure has been applied.
. *Produce the documents* — the price goes into the offer, transport is shown on its own line, and the Word file and PDF are created and filed.

NOTE: This standalone offer is purely a priced catalogue pick. It does not perform any firing-rate or fabrication calculation, and it does not add a pilot burner — those belong to the full preheater offers, where the burner is part of a larger system.

[NOTE]
.Worked example — standalone offer
====
Chosen burner = *ENCON 7A* (gas); catalogue price = ₹1,32,194; quantity 1; P&F 5 % + designing 10 % + negotiation 0 % (= +15 %).

* 1,32,194 × 1 × 1.15 = 1,52,023 → rounded to the nearest ₹1,000 = *₹1,52,000*

For 2 burners: 1,32,194 × 2 × 1.15 = 3,04,047 → *₹3,04,000*. Transport, if any, is added and then shown on its own line.

(GAIL pre-assembled sets are priced the same way — straight from the catalogue. Where a GAIL burner is built up from parts elsewhere in the system, the fabrication cost is multiplied by 1.25, but this standalone offer uses the ready catalogue price.)
====

== Full calculation reference (every step)

A standalone burner offer is a *catalogue pick* — no firing-rate calculation is done. But the same burner catalogue and the *selection rule* are what the preheater offers use internally to choose the right size, so both are documented here.

=== The burner catalogue

Burners come in four families, each pre-priced by size:

* *ENCON Gas Burner* — for NG / LPG / COG
* *ENCON Oil (Film) Burner* — for LDO / HSD / SKO
* *ENCON Dual-Fuel Burner* — gas + oil in one head
* *GAIL pre-assembled gas sets* — ready-built, named by kilowatt (e.g. "ENCON-500 KW")

Each ENCON size carries a *maximum firing capacity* (shown in litres/hour at 24″ W.G., or its kilowatt equivalent) and a *set price*. Sizes run from the small `2A`/`3A` up to `7A`, with `HV-` (high-velocity) variants in between. The top size, *ENCON 7A*, is the open-ended "and above" size — it carries a very large capacity so it is always picked when a single burner must carry a big load.

[cols="2,2,2,2",options="header"]
|===
| Gas size | Max firing (LPH) | ≈ Capacity | Set price ₹
| ENCON 2A   | 20    | 210 kW    | 35,158
| ENCON 3A   | 35    | 360 kW    | 35,758
| ENCON 4A   | 65    | 670 kW    | 43,162
| ENCON 5A   | 120   | 1,250 kW  | 67,059
| ENCON 6A   | 180   | 1,870 kW  | 70,290
| ENCON 7A   | (open-ended) | very large | 1,32,194
|===

(High-velocity `HV-3A … HV-7A` sizes sit between these on both capacity and price. Oil, dual-fuel and GAIL families have their own size/price lists.)

=== How a burner size is selected (inside the preheater offers)

The burner is chosen to *cover the required heat* at the burner pressure (24″ W.G. for a 28″ blower):

[cols="1,4,2",options="header"]
|===
| # | Step | Worked
| 1 | *Required heat* = gas flow × calorific value | 217.37 × 8,500 = *18,47,645 kcal/hr* (the default VLPH job)
| 2 | *Pick* the smallest ENCON size whose max firing covers that heat | 6A tops out at 16,06,500 kcal/hr (too small) → *ENCON 7A*
|===

* *Tundish per-burner example:* with the load split across 3 burners each carries 72.46 Nm³/hr → 72.46 × 8,500 = 6,15,910 kcal/hr → 4A (5,80,125) is too small → *ENCON 5A*. This is why a 3-burner tundish uses three small 5A burners instead of one big 7A.
* For *oil* fuels the size is matched on litres/hour instead of kcal/hr; for *dual-fuel* it must cover whichever fuel needs the larger burner.

=== Pricing (standalone offer)

[cols="3,3,2",options="header"]
|===
| Step | Formula | Worked
| Unit price | catalogue set price | ₹1,32,194 (ENCON 7A gas)
| Line total | unit × quantity | × 1 = ₹1,32,194
| Commercials | × (1 + P&F% + Designing% + Negotiation%) | × 1.15 = ₹1,52,023
| Final | round to nearest ₹1,000 (when any commercial applied) | *₹1,52,000*
|===

NOTE: This standalone offer is purely a priced catalogue pick — no firing-rate or fabrication calculation, and no pilot burner (those belong to the full preheater offers). The selection rule above only runs inside the preheaters. P&F, designing and negotiation are all *added* (negotiation does not discount); transport is added to the total and then shown on its own line.
