= Vertical Ladle Preheater (VLPH)
:description: What a Vertical Ladle Preheater offer needs, what it produces, and how its price is worked out — in plain language.

A vertical ladle preheater dries and heats the refractory lining of a steel ladle before molten metal is poured into it. A burner fires downward into the ladle from a hood on top, so the ladle is heated standing upright.

== What you enter

*The heating job* — either describe the process and let the system size the burner, or enter the burner size directly.

|Lining start temperature |650 °C
|Lining finish temperature |1200 °C
|Refractory weight |21,500 kg
|Heating time |2 hours
|Efficiency |0.52
|Ladle capacity |10 tons
|(Direct method) Burner capacity |in kW — used instead of the process figures
|(Direct method) Heat input |in kcal/hr — overrides the capacity if given

== What the offer contains

*A priced bill of materials*, grouped by section. Each line shows the item, quantity, make, unit price and total:

* *Combustion-air line* — compensator, pressure gauge with isolation valve, low-pressure switch; (on PLC) orifice plate and flow transmitter; control valve; butterfly valve; rotary joint; and, when ignition is included, pilot and UV ball valves with flexible hoses.
* *Fuel line* — the gas-train package (for NG / RLNG / LPG) or the individual valves and regulators (for mixed gas, coke-oven gas or blast-furnace gas); the oil-line items for oil fuels; and an air-gas regulator in manual, PLC+AGR or PID modes.
* *Purging line* (if selected) — the nitrogen purging set.
* *Pilot line* (if ignition is included) — the pilot gas line.
* *ENCON-built items* — the burner; the bearing, plummer block and shaft (or a swivel assembly); the fabricated structure; the air–gas pipeline; the blower; the ignition transformer, sequence controller, UV sensor and pilot burner (when auto-ignition is on); the oil pumping unit (for oil fuels); and the ceramic fibre.
* *Instruments and panel* — control panel, instrument ball valves, PLC or PID, ratio controller, temperature transmitter, hydraulic power pack, and PLC-with-HMI.
* *Totals* — bought-out subtotal, ENCON subtotal, and grand total.

*The offer document* (Word file plus PDF), containing:

* a *Scope of Supply* listing in words everything ENCON will provide;
* a *Price Schedule* showing the all-inclusive price, with transport on its own separate line;
* the editable *Terms & Conditions* (prices, delivery, taxes, payment, validity, guarantee);
* a *Make List* naming the supplier for each major item.

== How the price is worked out

. *Work out the fuel and air needed* from the heating figures (or the direct capacity you typed), adding a small safety margin.
. *Choose the equipment* — size the gas and air pipework, then pick the burner, gas train, air-gas regulator (where used), blower and valves from the catalogue to suit those flows.
. *Size the air line from the blower* that was actually selected, so the pipe, the valve and the displayed air flow all agree.
. *Fill in the structure and ceramic* automatically from the ladle size (overridable).
. *Price every line and total it up* — bought-out items summed and multiplied by the mark-up, plus ENCON-made items, giving the grand total.
. *Add the commercials* — packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation added as percentages (they add to the price, they do not discount it), plus flat transport, rounded to the nearest ₹1,000.
. *Produce the documents* — the all-inclusive price in the offer, transport shown on its own line, saved as Word and PDF.
