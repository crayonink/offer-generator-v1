= Vertical Ladle Preheater (VLPH)
:description: What a Vertical Ladle Preheater offer needs, what it produces, and how its price is worked out — in plain language.

A vertical ladle preheater dries and heats the refractory lining of a steel ladle before molten metal is poured into it. A burner fires downward into the ladle from a hood on top, so the ladle is heated standing upright.

TIP: If the lining is still hot when the ladle comes in (start temperature *above 600 °C*), it is already in circulation and only needs a top-up — the system automatically switches to *Circulating Ladle Preheater* mode and sizes the burner for *half* the refractory weight. See xref:products/vlph.adoc#_circulating_ladle_preheater[Circulating Ladle Preheater].

== What the offer contains

*A priced bill of materials*, grouped by section. Each line shows the item, quantity, make, unit price and total:

* *Combustion-air line* — compensator, pressure gauge with isolation valve, low-pressure switch; (on PLC) orifice plate and flow transmitter; control valve; butterfly valve; rotary joint; and, when ignition is included, pilot and UV ball valves with flexible hoses.
* *Fuel line* — the gas-train package (for NG / RLNG / LPG) or the individual valves and regulators (for mixed gas, coke-oven gas or blast-furnace gas); the oil-line items for oil fuels; and an air-gas regulator in manual, PLC+AGR or PID modes.
* *Purging line* (if selected) — the nitrogen purging set.
* *Pilot line* (if ignition is included) — the pilot gas line.
* *ENCON-built items* — the burner; the bearing, plummer block and shaft (or a swivel assembly); the fabricated structure; the air–gas pipeline; the blower; the ignition transformer, sequence controller, UV sensor and pilot burner (when auto-ignition is on); the oil pumping unit (for oil fuels); and the ceramic fibre.
* *Instruments and panel* — control panel, instrument ball valves, PLC or PID, ratio controller, temperature transmitter, hydraulic power pack, and PLC-with-HMI.
* *Totals* — bought-out subtotal, ENCON subtotal, and grand total.

*The offer document* (Word file plus PDF), containing:

* a *Scope of Supply* listing in words everything ENCON will provide;
* a *Price Schedule* showing the all-inclusive price, with transport on its own separate line;
* the editable *Terms & Conditions* (prices, delivery, taxes, payment, validity, guarantee);
* a *Make List* naming the supplier for each major item.

== How the price is worked out

. *Work out the fuel and air needed* from the heating figures (or the direct capacity you typed), adding a small safety margin.
. *Choose the equipment* — size the gas and air pipework, then pick the burner, gas train, air-gas regulator (where used), blower and valves from the catalogue to suit those flows.
. *Size the air line from the blower* that was actually selected, so the pipe, the valve and the displayed air flow all agree.
. *Fill in the structure and ceramic* automatically from the ladle size (overridable).
. *Price every line and total it up* — bought-out items summed and multiplied by the mark-up, plus ENCON-made items, giving the grand total.
. *Add the commercials* — packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation added as percentages (they add to the price, they do not discount it), plus flat transport, rounded to the nearest ₹1,000.
. *Produce the documents* — the all-inclusive price in the offer, transport shown on its own line, saved as Word and PDF.

.Worked example — start to finish (every number)
====
*Given inputs (defaults):* lining 650 → 1200 °C, refractory 21,500 kg, heat factor 0.25, efficiency 0.52, heating time 2 hr, natural gas at 8,500 kcal/Nm³, blower pressure 28″ WG, 10-ton ladle, automatic (PLC + AGR) control, single burner.

*Step 0 — Circulating check.* The start temperature Ti = 650 °C is *above 600 °C*, so this is a *Circulating Ladle Preheater*: the ladle is already hot and in circulation, so only *half* the refractory mass needs heating. Effective weight = 21,500 ÷ 2 = *10,750 kg*. Every figure below uses this effective weight. (A ladle starting at 600 °C or below would use the full 21,500 kg — see xref:products/vlph.adoc#_circulating_ladle_preheater[Circulating Ladle Preheater].)

*Step 1 — Fuel & air needed.*

* Average temperature to raise = (1200 − 650) ÷ 2 + 50 = *325 °C*
* Firing rate = *10,750* × 0.25 × 325 = *8,73,437.5 kcal* (uses the effective weight)
* Heat load = 8,73,437.5 ÷ 0.52 = *16,79,687.5 kcal*
* Fuel for the 2-hr cycle = 16,79,687.5 ÷ 8,500 = 197.61 Nm³ → ÷ 2 hr = 98.81 Nm³/hr → +10 % margin = *108.69 Nm³/hr* (gas flow)
* Firing rate in MW = 108.69 × 8,500 ÷ (860 × 1000) = *1.07 MW*
* Combustion air = 8,500 × 108.69 × 118 ÷ 1,00,000 = *1,090.12 Nm³/hr*
* Blower air = 1,090.12 ÷ 1.7 = *641.25 CFM*; reference HP at 28″ = 641.25 × 28 ÷ 3,200 = *5.61 HP*

*Step 2 — Equipment selected from the catalogue.*

* Burner = *ENCON 5A* (smallest ENCON gas burner whose max firing covers the flow at 24″ WG)
* Blower = *ENCON 28/7.5* — 7.5 HP, 1,450 Nm³/hr, 850 CFM (smallest in the 28″ series ≥ 5.61 HP)
* Gas train = *DN40 × DN40* (its flow band covers 108.69 Nm³/hr)
* Air-gas regulator included (automatic AGR control), sized from the gas-train outlet NB

*Step 3 — Pipe sizing.*

* Gas line: 108.69 Nm³/hr at 12.7 m/s → *65 NB*
* Air line: sized from the *selected blower's* airflow = 850 CFM × 1.7 = 1,445 Nm³/hr at 15 m/s → *200 NB*

*Step 4 — Structure & ceramic.* Auto-filled from the 10-ton ladle mapping (MS structure kg, ceramic-fibre roll count, air-gas pipeline kg); these feed the per-kg ENCON lines (fabrication ₹110/kg, pipeline ₹125/kg). These are *not* affected by the circulating halving — only the burner/blower/pipe sizing is.

*Step 5 — BOM totals* (every line priced from the pricelist + selectors):

* Bought-out items total = *₹7,15,018.25*
* ENCON-made items total = *₹10,71,055.32*

*Step 6 — Commercials* (mark-up 1.8, P&F 5 %, designing 5 %, negotiation 0 %):

* Grand Total = bought-out × 1.8 + ENCON = 7,15,018.25 × 1.8 + 10,71,055.32 = 12,87,032.85 + 10,71,055.32 = *₹23,58,088*
* + P&F 5 % + Designing 5 % (= +10 %) + Negotiation 0 % + Transport ₹0 = 25,93,897
* Round to nearest ₹1,000 = *₹25,94,000* (the single all-inclusive price; transport, if any, is then shown on its own line)

*Step 7 — Documents.* The ₹25,94,000 price, the scope of supply, the itemised BOM and the terms go into the offer, saved as Word + PDF and filed.

=== A. Sizing

==== Circulating Ladle Preheater

A *hot* ladle — one whose lining is still above *600 °C* when it comes in — is already in circulation between casts. It does not need its whole refractory mass reheated, only a top-up. So when the start temperature *Ti is greater than 600 °C*, the vertical preheater switches to *Circulating Ladle Preheater* mode and uses an *effective refractory weight of half the entered weight* for all the sizing below.

* *Trigger:* Ti > 600 °C (calculate-from-process mode only)
* *Effect:* effective weight = entered weight ÷ 2 — this halved figure feeds the firing rate, and therefore the burner, blower and pipe sizes
* *Not affected:* the structure, ceramic-fibre and pipeline quantities (those still come from the ladle-size mapping); only the heating calculation uses the halved weight
* *Shown before costing:* the calculation panel flags "Circulating Ladle Preheater" and lists the effective weight

*Example:* entered weight 21,500 kg at Ti 650 → effective *10,750 kg*. (The reference workbook's circulating case — 43,000 kg at Ti 800 → effective 21,500 kg → firing rate 13,43,750 kcal — matches exactly.) A ladle at 600 °C or below uses the full entered weight.

==== Average temperature to raise

This is the single number that drives the burner size, and it is picked by *two branches* depending on how hot the ladle is at the start (`Ti`):

[cols="2,3,3",options="header"]
|===
| Ladle state | Formula | Why
| *Warm start* — Ti > 100 °C (ladle already in cycle) | (Tf − Ti) / 2 + 50 | Take *half the temperature span* — heating ramps up rather than jumping straight to Tf — plus a *+50 °C* offset. A reheated ladle needs less energy than its absolute temperatures suggest, so this delta-based figure is more realistic than a plain mean.
| *Cold start* — Ti ≤ 100 °C (effectively from ambient) | (Tf + Ti) / 2 | A plain mean of start and finish temperatures.
|===

* *Warm-start example (defaults):* Ti 650, Tf 1200 → (1200 − 650) / 2 + 50 = 275 + 50 = *325 °C*
* *Cold-start example:* Ti 30, Tf 1200 → (1200 + 30) / 2 = *615 °C*

The `Ti > 100` threshold is the switch between "warm / in-cycle ladle" and "cold start". The warm-start branch deliberately yields a *smaller* rise (and thus a smaller burner) than a naive mean would.

[cols="1,4,2",options="header"]
|===
| # | Formula | Worked (defaults — circulating, Ti 650)

| 0 | *Effective weight* = entered weight ÷ 2 if Ti > 600 (circulating), else entered weight | 21,500 ÷ 2 = *10,750 kg*
| 1 | *Average temp rise* — warm start (Ti > 100): (Tf − Ti)/2 + 50; cold start (Ti ≤ 100): (Tf + Ti)/2 | (1200−650)/2 + 50 = *325 °C*
| 2 | *Firing rate* (kcal) = effective weight × heat factor × avg-temp-rise | 10,750 × 0.25 × 325 = *8,73,437.5*
| 3 | *Heat load* = firing rate ÷ efficiency | ÷ 0.52 = *16,79,687.5 kcal*
| 4 | *Fuel consumption* = heat load ÷ CV | ÷ 8,500 = *197.61 Nm³*
| 5 | *Calculated firing rate* = fuel ÷ time | ÷ 2 = *98.81 Nm³/hr*
| 6 | *+10 % margin* → gas flow | × 1.1 = *108.69 Nm³/hr*
| 7 | *Firing rate (MW)* = flow × CV ÷ (860 × 1000) | = *1.07 MW*
| 8 | *Combustion air* = CV × flow × 118 ÷ 1,00,000 | = *1,090.12 Nm³/hr*
| 9 | *CFM* = air ÷ 1.7; *blower HP* = CFM × pressure ÷ 3,200 | 641.25 CFM; *5.61 HP*
|===

*Direct mode* (instead of steps 0–6): heat = burner kW × 860 (or the entered heat input); gas flow = heat ÷ CV; air = heat × 118 ÷ 1,00,000. (Direct mode enters burner capacity, not weight, so the circulating halving does not apply.)

*Pipe sizing* (`pipes.py`) — area = (flow ÷ 3600) ÷ velocity; inner dia = √(4·area/π) × 1000, rounded up to the next standard NB:

* Gas line at 12.7 m/s → 65 NB; air line uses the *selected blower's* CFM × 1.7 (1,445 Nm³/hr) at 15 m/s → 200 NB. Oil-fuel line NB: ≤ 250 LPH → 20 NB, else 25 NB.

*Equipment selection* (`selection_engine.py`):

* *Burner* — smallest ENCON size whose max firing covers the gas flow at the burner pressure (here ENCON 5A).
* *Blower* — required HP = CFM × pressure ÷ 3,200; pick the smallest blower in the chosen pressure series at or above it (ENCON 28/7.5). (For oil, atomising-air CFM = equivalent LPH × 10; dual-fuel takes the larger air demand.)
* *Gas train* — the row whose [min, max] flow band brackets the gas flow (DN40 × DN40 for 108.69 Nm³/hr).
* *Air-gas regulator* — only for Manual / PLC+AGR / PID; sized from the gas-train outlet NB (Flanged ≥ 65 NB, else Threaded).
* *Butterfly & rotary joint* — sized to the air-line NB.

==== Combustion air, CFM and the air line

CFM appears twice in the sizing — once to *pick the blower*, once to *size the air pipe* — and the same number, *1.7*, links them. This subsection sets out the whole chain because it is the part most often misread.

*Why 1.7?* It is simply the *unit conversion* between the two ways of measuring airflow: imperial *CFM* (cubic feet per minute, how blowers are rated) and metric *Nm³/hr* (normal cubic metres per hour, how the combustion calc produces the air figure). One CFM ≈ 1.7 Nm³/hr. So:

* Nm³/hr → CFM: *divide by 1.7*
* CFM → Nm³/hr: *multiply by 1.7*

*Step 1 — required air → CFM → blower.* The combustion-air figure from step 8 is in Nm³/hr, but blowers are catalogued in CFM, so it is converted first:

* *Gas CFM* = combustion air ÷ 1.7 → 1,090.12 ÷ 1.7 = *641.25 CFM*
* *Oil CFM* (atomising air, oil fuels only) = equivalent LPH × 10
* *Dual-fuel* → take the *larger* of the gas and oil CFM
* *Required HP* = CFM × blower pressure (″WG) ÷ 3,200 → 641.25 × 28 ÷ 3,200 = *5.61 HP*
* The blower is the *smallest catalogue model in the chosen pressure series at or above that HP* → ENCON 28/7.5 (7.5 HP, rated 850 CFM / 1,450 Nm³/hr)

*Step 2 — chosen blower → air-line flow → pipe NB.* The air pipe is **not** sized from the raw 1,090 Nm³/hr requirement. It is sized from the airflow the *selected* blower actually moves — its *rated* CFM converted back to Nm³/hr:

* *Air-line flow* = selected blower CFM × 1.7 → 850 × 1.7 = *1,445 Nm³/hr* (this is the "Air Flow" shown on screen)
* That flow runs through the pipe formula at 15 m/s → *200 NB*
* The result is floored: *air-line NB = max(125 NB, the pipe-formula NB, the air-duct NB)* — the line is never smaller than 125 NB

*Why the round-trip?* Because the chosen blower is always one size *up* from the bare requirement (7.5 HP / 850 CFM for a 5.61 HP / 641 CFM need), it actually delivers *more* air than the minimum. Sizing the pipe from the blower's real output — rather than the smaller required figure — keeps the displayed Air Flow and the pipe diameter consistent, and ensures the line can carry everything the blower pushes. The butterfly valve and rotary joint then follow this same air-line NB.

=== B. Costing (`bom/vlph_builder.py`)

Each `_row` is priced either by a computed override or an exact pricelist lookup:

* *Fabrication / structure* = MS structure kg × ₹110/kg
* *Air-gas pipeline* = pipeline kg × ₹125/kg
* *Ceramic fibre* = roll count × ceramic rate
* *Burner, blower, gas train, AGR, valves, HPU* = the prices returned by the selectors / pricelist
* *Bought-out instruments & panel* (compensator, pressure gauge, switch, orifice, DPT, control valve, PLC, control panel, hydraulic power pack, etc.) = exact pricelist lookups
* Rows are split into *bought-out* (everything not tagged "ENCON ITEMS", excluding the ratio controller) and *ENCON* items.
