= Recuperators
:description: What a Recuperator offer needs, what it produces, and how its price is worked out — in plain language.

A recuperator recovers heat from a furnace's hot waste flue gas and uses it to pre-heat the incoming combustion air. Pre-heated air means less fuel is burned, so the recuperator pays for itself in fuel savings. Unlike the catalogue products, a recuperator is *sized from the heat duty* — the system works out how big the tube bundle needs to be, then prices the steelwork.

== What the offer contains

*The sizing results* — the heat to be recovered, the required heat-transfer surface area, the number of tubes and how they are arranged into rows and columns, the bank length and width, and the weights of the tubes and of the supporting steelwork.

*A priced bill of materials* —

* the hot-bank tubes and the cold-bank tubes (priced by weight at the stainless- or mild-steel rate);
* the side-hood fabrication;
* the combustion-air inlet assembly;
* the structural steel (channels and angles);
* the pipe-bending, welding and drilling labour;
* the thermocouple;
* and the bought-out, ENCON and grand totals.

*The offer document* (Word + PDF), containing:

* a *Scope of Supply / Materials of Construction* table describing the tubes (hot bank bent, cold bank straight) and the steel grades;
* a *Price Schedule* — either a single all-inclusive line or every item in full, as chosen;
* a *Designing Parameters* table echoing the flue and air conditions, the surface area and the tube geometry;
* optional 3-D images of the recuperator;
* the *Terms & Conditions.*

== How the price is worked out

. *Heat balance.* From the air flow and the temperature rise, work out how much heat must be recovered, and what temperature the flue gas drops to after giving up that heat.
. *Surface area.* Using the average temperature difference between the two streams and the heat-transfer coefficient, work out the heat-transfer surface area required.
. *Tube count and layout.* Convert the surface area into a number of tubes, then arrange them into a near-square grid of rows and columns, split evenly between the hot and cold banks.
. *Dimensions and weights.* From the grid, work out the bank length and width, and the weight of the tubes and of all the supporting steelwork.
. *Price the materials.* Price the tubes by weight, price the fabricated assemblies and structural steel, and add the bending, welding and drilling labour and the thermocouple — giving the grand total.
. *Add the commercials.* Apply the cost-to-sale mark-up, then add packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation as percentages and the flat transport, rounding up to the nearest ₹1,000.
. *Produce the documents* — the price and all the sizing results go into the offer, which is saved as Word and PDF and filed.

[NOTE]
.Worked example — start to finish (every number)
====
*Given inputs:* flue gas 1,900 Nm³/hr at 800 °C; combustion air 1,750 Nm³/hr to be heated 35 → 400 °C; cp,air 0.247, cp,flue 0.23; heat-transfer coefficient U = 30; SS304 tubes both banks. (All rates are the pricelist defaults.)

*Step 1 — Heat balance.*

* Air mass = 1,750 × 1.2 = *2,100 kg/hr*
* Heat to recover, Q = 2,100 × 0.247 × (400 − 35) = 2,100 × 0.247 × 365 = *1,89,325.5 kcal/hr*
* Flue mass = 1,900 × 1.2 = *2,280 kg/hr*
* Flue-gas drop = Q ÷ (flue mass × cp,flue) = 1,89,325.5 ÷ (2,280 × 0.23) = 1,89,325.5 ÷ 524.4 = 361.0 °C
* Final flue temp = 800 − 361.0 = *438.97 °C*

*Step 2 — Surface area.*

* dT1 = 800 − 400 = 400; dT2 = 438.97 − 35 = 403.97
* LMTD (raw) = (400 − 403.97) ÷ ln(400 ÷ 403.97) = (−3.97) ÷ (−0.009876) = 402.0; × 0.9 factor = *361.78 °C*
* Surface area A = Q ÷ (LMTD × U) = 1,89,325.5 ÷ (361.78 × 30) = 1,89,325.5 ÷ 10,853.4 = *17.44 m²*

*Step 3 — Tube count & grid.*

* Raw tubes = A ÷ (3.14 × 0.0483 × (0.63 + 0.1)) = 17.44 ÷ (3.14 × 0.0483 × 0.73) = 17.44 ÷ 0.110726 = 157.5
* Target = ⌈157.5⌉ = 158 → near-square even-column grid = *14 rows × 12 cols = 168 tubes*
* Pipes per bank = 168 ÷ 2 = *84*

*Step 4 — Dimensions & weights.*

* Bank length = (6.5 × 32) + (7 × 48.3) + 150 = 208 + 338.1 + 150 = *696.1 mm*
* Bank width = (6 × 48.3) + (5.5 × 32) + 150 = 289.8 + 176 + 150 = *615.8 mm*
* Weight per pipe = 3.16 × 0.63 = 1.9908 kg → hot bank = cold bank = 1.9908 × 84 = *167.23 kg* each
* MS structural weights: outer shell *81.32 kg*, air-inlet duct *110.21 kg*, hot-outlet duct *121.44 kg*, pipe-holding plate *525.75 kg*, bottom box *160.93 kg*

*Step 5 — Price the materials* (each line = qty/weight × rate):

* SS304 tube — Hot bank: 167.23 kg × ₹250 = *₹41,807.50*
* SS304 tube — Cold bank: 167.23 kg × ₹250 = *₹41,807.50*
* MS Side Hood fabrication (flat) = *₹75,000*
* MS Combustion Air Inlet Assembly = (81.32 + 110.21 + 121.44 + 525.75 + 160.93 + 100 flanges + 1,500 hood) × ₹70/kg = 2,599.65 × 70 = *₹1,81,975.50*
* MS Channel 150×75×10: 10 × 17 × ₹51 = *₹8,670*
* MS Angle 65×25: 25 × 8.8 × ₹51 = *₹11,220*
* MS Angle 75×10: 9 × 10 × ₹51 = *₹4,590*
* MS Angle 50×15: 4.5 × 15 × ₹51 = *₹3,442.50*
* Pipe Bending: (14 + 12) × ₹350 = *₹9,100*
* Welding Rods: 168 × 4 × ₹8 = *₹5,376*
* Hole Fabrication: 168 × 2 × ₹100 = *₹33,600*
* Thermocouple with TT (flat, the only bought-out line) = *₹15,000*
* Bought-out total = *₹15,000* (the thermocouple)
* ENCON total = *₹4,16,589*
* *Grand total = 15,000 + 4,16,589 = ₹4,31,589*

*Step 6 — Commercials.*

* Sale = Grand × 1.8 = 4,31,589 × 1.8 = *₹7,76,860*
* Final = Sale × (1 + designing 0.10 + negotiation 0.10) = 7,76,860 × 1.20 = 9,32,232 → round *up* to nearest ₹1,000 = *₹9,33,000*
* (If P&F % or a flat Transport are entered in Step 3, they add on top before the round-up.)

*Step 7 — Documents.* The final price (₹9,33,000) plus all the sizing results above go into the offer, saved as Word + PDF and filed.
====

=== A. Sizing — `calculations/recup.py`

[cols="1,4,2",options="header"]
|===
| # | Formula | Worked (defaults)

| 1 (E4) | *Flue mass* = flue_flow × 1.2 | 1,900 × 1.2 = *2,280 kg/hr*
| 2 (E13) | *Heat to recover* Q = (air_vol × 1.2) × cp,air × (T_air,out − T_air,in) | 2,100 × 0.247 × 365 = *1,89,325.5 kcal/hr*
| 3 (E7) | *Final flue temp* = T_flue,in − Q ÷ (flue_mass × cp,flue) | 800 − 189325.5 ÷ (2,280 × 0.23) = *438.97 °C*
| 4 (E14) | *LMTD*: dT1 = T_flue,in − T_air,out; dT2 = T_flue,out − T_air,in; LMTD = ((dT1 − dT2) ÷ ln(dT1/dT2)) × 0.9 | dT1 = 400, dT2 = 403.97 → 402.0 × 0.9 = *361.78 °C* (if dT1≤0, dT2≤0 or dT1=dT2, falls back to the arithmetic mean)
| 5 (E15) | *Surface area* A = Q ÷ (LMTD × U) | 189325.5 ÷ (361.78 × 30) = *17.44 m²*
| 6 (E19) | *Raw pipe count* = A ÷ (3.14 × (dia/1000) × (length + 0.1)) — literal 3.14, matching the sheet | 17.44 ÷ (3.14 × 0.0483 × 0.73) = *157.5*
| 7 (E20/E21) | *Grid*: target = override, else ⌈raw⌉. `_best_grid` searches rows in [√n−8, √n+8], cols = ⌈n/rows⌉ forced *even*, scoring `aspect% + 3×waste%`, tie-break toward rows = 14 | ⌈157.5⌉ = 158 → *14 rows × 12 cols = 168 tubes*
| 8 | *Pipes per bank* = total ÷ 2 | 168 ÷ 2 = *84*
| 9 (E16) | *Bank length* = ((rows−1)/2)×pitch + (rows/2)×dia + margin | (6.5×32) + (7×48.3) + 150 = *696.1 mm*
| 10 (E17) | *Bank width* = (cols/2)×48.3 + ((cols−1)/2)×pitch + margin | (6×48.3) + (5.5×32) + 150 = *615.8 mm*
| 11 (E27) | *Weight per pipe* = kg/m × length | 3.16 × 0.63 = *1.9908 kg*
| 12 (E28/E35) | *Hot bank = Cold bank* = per-pipe × pipes-per-bank | 1.9908 × 84 = *167.23 kg* each (total 334.46 kg)
|===
