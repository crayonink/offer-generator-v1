= Tundish Preheating System
:description: What a Tundish Preheating offer needs, what it produces, and how its price is worked out — in plain language.

A tundish preheating system heats the refractory lining of a tundish (the vessel that feeds molten steel into a continuous caster) before it goes into service. It works just like the xref:products/vlph.adoc[vertical ladle preheater], with one important difference: a tundish is usually heated by *several burners at once*.

== What you enter

The same details as the vertical ladle preheater:

|Refractory weight |21,500 kg
|Heating time |2 hours
|Efficiency |0.52

[cols="3,4"]
|===
|What you enter |Typical value / options

|Number of burners |1 — the total firing rate is split between them, and the burner-related items are multiplied by this number
|Structure-weight override |kg (leave 0 to fill in automatically)
|Ceramic-fibre override |number of rolls (leave 0 to fill in automatically)

And the commercial figures — bought-out mark-up (1.8), packaging & forwarding (5 %), designing (5 %), negotiation (0 %), and a flat transport amount.

== What the offer contains

The same priced bill of materials and offer document (Word + PDF) as the ladle preheaters:

* *The burner* and its pilot and ignition parts — counted once *per burner*.
* *The shared equipment* — blower, gas train, air-gas regulator / control valve, butterfly and shut-off valves, gate valve, pressure gauges and switches, rotary joint, structure, air–gas pipeline and ceramic fibre — counted once.
* *The oil pumping unit* for oil fuels.
* *Totals* — bought-out, ENCON and grand total.

The offer document carries the Scope of Supply (the equipment is named "Tundish Preheater"), the Price Schedule with transport on its own line, the editable Terms & Conditions, and the Make List. It is produced from the same standard offer document as the other preheaters.

== How the price is worked out

. *Work out the total fuel and air needed* from the heating figures or the direct capacity.
. *Split the firing rate across the burners* — the shared equipment is sized for the total flow, but each burner is sized for its own share.
. *Choose the equipment* — pipework, burner, gas train, regulator, blower and valves from the catalogue.
. *Size the air line from the chosen blower's airflow.*
. *Fill in the structure and ceramic figures* from the size, or use your overrides.
. *Price every line and total it up*, multiplying the per-burner items by the number of burners.
. *Add the commercials* — packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation as percentages, plus flat transport, rounded to the nearest ₹1,000.
. *Produce the documents* — the all-inclusive price in the offer, transport on its own line, saved as Word and PDF.

[NOTE]
.Worked example — start to finish (3 burners, every number)
====
*Given inputs:* lining 650 → 1200 °C, refractory 21,500 kg, heat factor 0.25, efficiency 0.52, heating time 2 hr, natural gas at 8,500 kcal/Nm³, blower pressure 28″ WG, 10-ton, automatic (PLC + AGR) control, *3 burners*.

*Step 1 — Total fuel & air* (worked exactly as for the xref:products/vlph.adoc[vertical preheater]):

* Average temperature to raise = (1200 − 650) ÷ 2 + 50 = *325 °C*
* Firing rate = 21,500 × 0.25 × 325 = *17,46,875 kcal* → heat load ÷ 0.52 = *33,59,375 kcal*
* Total gas = 33,59,375 ÷ 8,500 ÷ 2 hr × 1.1 margin = *217.37 Nm³/hr*; firing rate = *2.15 MW*
* Total combustion air = 8,500 × 217.37 × 118 ÷ 1,00,000 = *2,180.23 Nm³/hr*

*Step 2 — Split across the 3 burners.* This is the tundish difference:

* Each burner is sized for 217.37 ÷ 3 = *72.46 Nm³/hr* of gas (and 2,180.23 ÷ 3 = 726.74 Nm³/hr of air)
* The *shared* equipment — blower, gas train, main air & gas lines — is still sized for the *full* 217.37 / 2,180.23 Nm³/hr
* The burner is re-selected for the *per-burner* flow → *ENCON 5A* (vs ENCON 7A if one burner carried the whole load), then counted *3 times* in the BOM along with its pilot/ignition parts

*Step 3 — Equipment selected:*

* Burner = *ENCON 5A × 3*; shared Blower = *ENCON 28/15* (15 HP, 2,890 Nm³/hr, 1,700 CFM); shared Gas train = *DN50 × DN65* (total-flow band)

*Step 4 — Pipe sizing* (from total flow):

* Gas line: 217.37 Nm³/hr at 12.7 m/s → *80 NB*; air line: blower 1,700 CFM × 1.7 = 2,890 Nm³/hr at 15 m/s → *300 NB*

*Step 5 — BOM totals* (per-burner items × 3, shared items × 1):

* Bought-out items total = *₹8,30,900.90*
* ENCON-made items total = *₹11,23,855.32*

*Step 6 — Commercials* (mark-up 1.8, P&F 5 %, designing 5 %, negotiation 0 %):

* Grand Total = 8,30,900.90 × 1.8 + 11,23,855.32 = 14,95,621.62 + 11,23,855.32 = *₹26,19,477*
* + P&F 5 % + Designing 5 % (= +10 %) + Transport ₹0 = 28,81,424
* Round up to nearest ₹1,000 = *₹28,81,000* (the single all-inclusive price; transport, if any, on its own line)
====

== Full calculation reference (every step)

The Tundish Preheater uses the *same sizing math* as the xref:products/vlph.adoc[vertical ladle preheater] (`calculations/burner.py`, `calculations/pipes.py`, `bom/selectors/selection_engine.py`). The one structural difference is *multiple burners*: the shared equipment is sized for the total flow, each burner for its share, and the burner-related lines are counted once per burner. This section documents every formula with the 3-burner worked values above.

=== A. Sizing

==== Average temperature to raise

The number that drives the burner size is picked by *two branches* depending on how hot the tundish lining is at the start (`Ti`):

[cols="2,3,3",options="header"]
|===
| Lining state | Formula | Why
| *Warm start* — Ti > 100 °C (already in cycle) | (Tf − Ti) / 2 + 50 | Take *half the temperature span* — heating ramps up rather than jumping straight to Tf — plus a *+50 °C* offset. A reheated lining needs less energy than its absolute temperatures suggest, so this delta-based figure is more realistic than a plain mean.
| *Cold start* — Ti ≤ 100 °C (from ambient) | (Tf + Ti) / 2 | A plain mean of start and finish temperatures.
|===

* *Warm-start example (defaults):* Ti 650, Tf 1200 → (1200 − 650) / 2 + 50 = *325 °C*
* *Cold-start example:* Ti 30, Tf 1200 → (1200 + 30) / 2 = *615 °C*

[cols="1,4,2",options="header"]
|===
| # | Formula | Worked (total)

| 1 | *Average temp rise* — warm (Ti > 100): (Tf − Ti)/2 + 50; cold (Ti ≤ 100): (Tf + Ti)/2 | (1200−650)/2 + 50 = *325 °C*
| 2 | *Firing rate* (kcal) = weight × heat factor × avg-temp-rise | 21,500 × 0.25 × 325 = *17,46,875*
| 3 | *Heat load* = firing rate ÷ efficiency | ÷ 0.52 = *33,59,375 kcal*
| 4 | *Fuel consumption* = heat load ÷ CV | ÷ 8,500 = *395.22 Nm³*
| 5 | *Calculated firing rate* = fuel ÷ time | ÷ 2 = *197.61 Nm³/hr*
| 6 | *+10 % margin* → total gas flow | × 1.1 = *217.37 Nm³/hr*
| 7 | *Firing rate (MW)* = flow × CV ÷ (860 × 1000) | = *2.15 MW*
| 8 | *Combustion air* = CV × flow × 118 ÷ 1,00,000 | = *2,180.23 Nm³/hr*
| 9 | *CFM* = air ÷ 1.7; *blower HP* = CFM × pressure ÷ 3,200 | 1,282.49 CFM; *11.22 HP*
|===

==== Multi-burner handling

This is the only thing that sets the tundish apart from the ladle preheaters:

* *Direct mode* takes the burner capacity as *per burner*: total heat = burner kW × 860 × number-of-burners. (An entered total heat-input figure overrides this.)
* The *blower, gas train and main lines* are selected and sized for the *total* gas/air flow.
* The *burner* is then re-selected for the *per-burner* flow (total ÷ number-of-burners) — here 72.46 Nm³/hr → *ENCON 5A* instead of the 7A a single burner would need.
* In the BOM, the burner and its pilot/ignition parts are *counted once per burner*; everything shared is counted once.

*Pipe sizing* (`pipes.py`) — area = (flow ÷ 3600) ÷ velocity; inner dia = √(4·area/π) × 1000, rounded up to the next standard NB. Gas line at 12.7 m/s → 80 NB; air line uses the selected blower's CFM × 1.7 (2,890 Nm³/hr) at 15 m/s → 300 NB. (Pipes use the *total* flow — they feed the shared mains.)

=== B. Costing (`bom/vlph_builder.py`, tundish mode)

Each `_row` is priced by a computed override or an exact pricelist lookup:

* *Fabrication / structure* = MS structure kg × ₹110/kg
* *Air-gas pipeline* = pipeline kg × ₹125/kg
* *Ceramic fibre* = roll count × ceramic rate
* *Burner + pilot + ignition* = selector price × *number of burners*
* *Blower, gas train, AGR, valves, rotary joint, HPU* = selector / pricelist prices, counted once
* *Bought-out instruments & panel* = exact pricelist lookups
* Rows are split into *bought-out* and *ENCON* items.
