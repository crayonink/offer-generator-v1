= Tundish Preheating System
:description: What a Tundish Preheating offer needs, what it produces, and how its price is worked out — in plain language.

A tundish preheating system heats the refractory lining of a tundish (the holding vessel that feeds molten steel into a continuous caster) before it goes into service. It works just like the xref:products/vlph.adoc[vertical ladle preheater], with one important difference: a tundish is usually heated by *several burners at once* rather than a single burner. This page explains the inputs, the output, and how the price is built, and points out the few tundish-specific extras.

== What you enter

The same details as the vertical ladle preheater — the heating job, the fuel, the control system, the makes, the air and pipework, and the commercial figures — plus three tundish-specific items:

* *Number of burners* — how many burners share the heating. The total firing rate is split between them to size each burner, and the burner-related items in the bill of materials are multiplied by this number.
* *Structure-weight override* — type in the MS structure weight to use for the fabrication cost, instead of the figure the system would look up.
* *Ceramic-fibre override* — type in the number of ceramic-fibre rolls, instead of the looked-up figure.

== What the offer contains

The same priced bill of materials and offer document (Word + PDF) as the ladle preheaters:

* the burner (and its pilot and ignition parts) counted once per burner;
* the shared equipment — blower, gas train, valves, structure, pipeline, ceramic fibre — counted once;
* a Scope of Supply, a Price Schedule with transport on its own line, and editable Terms & Conditions;
* the equipment is named "Tundish Preheater" in the document.

The tundish offer is produced from the same standard offer document as the other preheaters — there is no separate tundish template.

== How the price is worked out

. *Work out the total fuel and air needed* from the heating figures or the direct capacity.
. *Split the firing rate across the burners.* The shared equipment (blower, gas train, main line) is sized for the total flow, but each burner is sized for its own share.
. *Choose the equipment* — pipework, burner, gas train, regulator, blower and valves from the catalogue.
. *Size the air line from the chosen blower's airflow.*
. *Fill in the structure and ceramic figures* from the ladle/tundish size, or use your overrides.
. *Price every line and total it up*, multiplying the per-burner items by the number of burners.
. *Add the commercials* — packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation as percentages, plus flat transport, rounded to the nearest ₹1,000.
. *Produce the documents* — the all-inclusive price in the offer, transport on its own line, saved as Word and PDF.
