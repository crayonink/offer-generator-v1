= Tundish Preheating System
:description: What a Tundish Preheating offer needs, what it produces, and how its price is worked out — in plain language.

A tundish preheating system heats the refractory lining of a tundish (the vessel that feeds molten steel into a continuous caster) before it goes into service. It works just like the xref:products/vlph.adoc[vertical ladle preheater], with one important difference: a tundish is usually heated by *several burners at once*.

== What you enter

The same details as the vertical ladle preheater:

[cols="3,4"]
|===
|What you enter |Typical value / options

|Sizing method |"Calculate from process" (default) or "Enter burner size directly"
|Lining start / finish temperature |650 °C / 1200 °C
|Refractory weight |21,500 kg
|Heating time |2 hours
|Efficiency |0.52
|Ladle / tundish capacity |10 tons
|(Direct) Burner capacity |kW, taken as the size *per burner*
|(Direct) Heat input |kcal/hr (overrides capacity)
|Main fuel + calorific value |Natural Gas (default) / LPG / COG / BFG / Mixed Gas / RLNG / oils; 8,500 kcal/Nm³
|Second fuel + its calorific value |None (default), or any of the above for dual-fuel
|Control mode / automatic type |Automatic (default) or Manual; PLC / PLC+AGR / PID
|Makes |control valve, shut-off valve, butterfly valve, pressure gauge
|Blower pressure |28 (default) or 40 inches water gauge
|Air–gas pipeline weight |1,000 kg
|Oil pumping unit type (oil fuels) |Simplex / Duplex 1 (default) / Duplex 2
|Purging line |No (default) or Yes
|===

Plus three tundish-specific items:

[cols="3,4"]
|===
|What you enter |Typical value / options

|Number of burners |1 — the total firing rate is split between them, and the burner-related items are multiplied by this number
|Structure-weight override |kg (leave 0 to fill in automatically)
|Ceramic-fibre override |number of rolls (leave 0 to fill in automatically)
|===

And the commercial figures — bought-out mark-up (1.8), packaging & forwarding (5 %), designing (5 %), negotiation (0 %), and a flat transport amount.

== What the offer contains

The same priced bill of materials and offer document (Word + PDF) as the ladle preheaters:

* *The burner* and its pilot and ignition parts — counted once *per burner*.
* *The shared equipment* — blower, gas train, air-gas regulator / control valve, butterfly and shut-off valves, gate valve, pressure gauges and switches, rotary joint, structure, air–gas pipeline and ceramic fibre — counted once.
* *The oil pumping unit* for oil fuels.
* *Totals* — bought-out, ENCON and grand total.

The offer document carries the Scope of Supply (the equipment is named "Tundish Preheater"), the Price Schedule with transport on its own line, the editable Terms & Conditions, and the Make List. It is produced from the same standard offer document as the other preheaters.

== How the price is worked out

. *Work out the total fuel and air needed* from the heating figures or the direct capacity.
. *Split the firing rate across the burners* — the shared equipment is sized for the total flow, but each burner is sized for its own share.
. *Choose the equipment* — pipework, burner, gas train, regulator, blower and valves from the catalogue.
. *Size the air line from the chosen blower's airflow.*
. *Fill in the structure and ceramic figures* from the size, or use your overrides.
. *Price every line and total it up*, multiplying the per-burner items by the number of burners.
. *Add the commercials* — packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation as percentages, plus flat transport, rounded to the nearest ₹1,000.
. *Produce the documents* — the all-inclusive price in the offer, transport on its own line, saved as Word and PDF.

[NOTE]
.Worked example — natural gas, 3 burners
====
The total heat and flow are worked out exactly as for the xref:products/vlph.adoc[vertical preheater]. For the same job (lining 650 → 1200 °C, 21,500 kg, 2 hours, NG at 8,500 kcal/Nm³) the total is:

* Total gas ≈ *217 Nm³/hr*, total combustion air ≈ *2,180 Nm³/hr* (with the 10 % margin)

Now the tundish split across *3 burners*:

* Each burner is sized for 217 ÷ 3 ≈ *72 Nm³/hr* of gas
* The *shared* equipment — blower, gas train, main line — is still sized for the full *217 Nm³/hr*
* In the bill of materials, the burner and its pilot/ignition parts are *counted 3 times*; the shared equipment is counted once

*Pricing:* the bought-out total (now including 3 burners) is multiplied by 1.8, the ENCON-made items added, and P&F / designing / negotiation and transport applied, rounded to the nearest ₹1,000.
====
