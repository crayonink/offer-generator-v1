= Heating & Pumping Unit (HPU)
:description: What a Heating & Pumping Unit offer needs, what it produces, and how its price is worked out — in plain language.

A heating and pumping unit takes fuel oil, *heats* it so it flows and atomises properly, and *pumps* it to the burner at the right pressure. It is the oil-handling skid between the oil tank and the burner. (For oils that are already hot enough and only need pumping, see the xref:products/pu.adoc[Pumping Unit].)

== What you enter

[cols="3,4"]
|===
|What you enter |Typical value / options

|Variant |Simplex (single), Duplex 1 (default), or Duplex 2 (with standby)
|Capacity |9 kW (choices: 3, 6, 9, 12, 16, 20, 24, 30, 36, 48)
|Oil flow rate |filled in automatically from the capacity and variant — you don't type it
|Quantity |1 (number of units)
|Packaging & forwarding |5 %
|Designing |10 %
|Negotiation |0 %
|Transport |flat ₹ amount (default 0)
|Customer details and Terms & Conditions |company, contacts, subject, and the standard terms
|===

There is no fuel drop-down or combustion setting here — this is purely the oil heating-and-pumping skid.

== What the offer contains

The offer document (Word + PDF) includes:

* *A scope section headed "Oil Heating and Pumping Unit"* describing a heating-and-pumping skid of the chosen variant, with bullets listing:
** the pump(s), each with its electric motor (two for Duplex, one for Simplex);
** an electric oil pre-heater with thermostatic control (two for Duplex 2, otherwise one);
** duplex coarse and fine filters (cold and hot side);
** a pressure-regulating valve;
** a pressure gauge and a temperature gauge.
* *A price schedule* with a single all-inclusive line. Transport, if entered, appears on its own separate line. Packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation are built into the unit price, not shown as separate lines.
* *The grand total* in figures and words.
* *The Terms & Conditions.*

A running total updates live as you change the inputs, and the finished offer can be previewed before sending.

== How the price is worked out

. *Look up the oil flow rate* for the chosen capacity and variant.
. *Build the unit price* by adding up the catalogue costs of the heating-and-pumping unit's parts and applying the standard mark-up.
. *Multiply by the quantity.*
. *Add the commercials* — packaging & forwarding, designing and negotiation are each added as a percentage (they add to the price, they do not discount it), and the flat transport is added.
. *Round* the total to the nearest ₹1,000 when any commercial figure has been applied.
. *Produce the documents* — the all-inclusive price goes into the offer, transport is shown on its own line, and the Word file and PDF are created and filed.

[NOTE]
.Worked example — default unit
====
Chosen unit = *9 kW, Duplex 1* (model HPD-9, oil flow 120 LPH). Its parts (pumps, motors, heater, filters, valves, gauges) add up to a material cost that, with the standard 1.8 mark-up, gives a unit price of *₹1,13,798*.

* Quantity 1, P&F 5 % + designing 10 % + negotiation 0 % (= +15 %): 1,13,798 × 1.15 = 1,30,867 → rounded to the nearest ₹1,000 = *₹1,31,000*

Transport, if any, is added to that total and then shown on its own line in the price schedule.
====

== Full calculation reference (every step)

A heating-and-pumping unit offer is built from a *parts list* held in the catalogue. The unit price is the sum of its parts times the standard mark-up; everything else is the same commercial chain as the other offers. This section documents every step with the real catalogue figures.

=== The three variants

[cols="2,4",options="header"]
|===
| Variant | What's in it
| *Simplex* (model HPS-) | One pump + motor, one electric oil pre-heater — no standby
| *Duplex 1* (model HPD-, default) | *Two* pumps + motors (one running, one standby), one pre-heater
| *Duplex 2* (model HPDD-) | Two pumps + motors *and two* pre-heaters — full redundancy
|===

The variant decides how many pumps and heaters are in the parts list, which is why Duplex costs more than Simplex for the same capacity.

=== How the unit price is built

[cols="1,5",options="header"]
|===
| # | Step
| 1 | *Add up the parts.* Sum the catalogue cost of every part in the chosen variant — pumps, motors, pre-heater(s), duplex coarse & fine filters, pressure-regulating valve, pressure and temperature gauges, and the skid fabrication.
| 2 | *Use live rates for raw materials.* Parts that are raw material (channels, plates, sheet, pipe, flats, fasteners) are re-costed at the current Rs/kg rates, so a pricebook update flows straight through to the unit price. Bought-out parts use their stored catalogue cost.
| 3 | *Apply the mark-up.* Unit price = material cost × *1.8*. (This is the figure in the table above.)
|===

=== Selection inside the preheater offers

When an oil-fuelled preheater needs an oil skid, the unit is picked automatically: take the burner's oil demand in litres/hour and choose the *smallest capacity whose flow rate covers it*. For example a burner needing 200 LPH picks the *16 kW* unit (200 LPH); 210 LPH would step up to the *20 kW* (250 LPH).

=== Pricing (standalone offer)

[cols="3,3,2",options="header"]
|===
| Step | Formula | Worked
| Unit price | parts cost × 1.8 | ₹1,13,798 (9 kW Duplex 1)
| Line total | unit × quantity | × 1 = ₹1,13,798
| Commercials | × (1 + P&F% + Designing% + Negotiation%) | × 1.15 = ₹1,30,867
| Final | round to nearest ₹1,000 (when any commercial applied) | *₹1,31,000*
|===
