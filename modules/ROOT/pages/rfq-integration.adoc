= RFQ Tracker Integration
:description: How the Offer Generator connects to the RFQ Enquiry Tracker.

_Status: proposed — documented, not yet built._

== Why integrate

ENCON runs two related tools. The *RFQ Enquiry Tracker* logs every incoming
request for quotation and follows it through to an offer — it records the
company, the contact, the item, who it is assigned to, the target date, and,
once an offer exists, the offer number, offer date, document and costing. The
*Offer Generator* (this tool) is what actually produces that priced offer.

Today the two are used side by side. The team logs an enquiry in the tracker,
then separately opens the generator, re-keys the customer and the item,
produces the offer, and goes back to the tracker to type in the offer number,
date, document link and costing. Connecting the two removes that double entry
and keeps the tracker up to date on its own.

== How they connect

The two apps stay separate — each keeps its own database and its own
deployment — and are joined loosely through a web link and a small interface.
Neither app's internal workings change; they simply hand information to each
other at two points. This keeps the integration low-risk and lets either app
be updated on its own.

== The flow

image::rfq-integration.svg[RFQ Tracker and Offer Generator integration]

. *Start from the enquiry.* On an enquiry in the tracker, a "Generate Offer"
  link opens the Offer Generator with that enquiry's details already filled
  in — company, contact, mobile, email, the item, and the sales and technical
  owners — along with the RFQ reference.
. *Produce the offer.* The estimator completes the technical inputs and
  generates the priced offer as normal: the Word and PDF documents and the
  costing sheet, filed to the shared drive.
. *Write the result back.* As the offer is finished, the generator sends the
  key results back to the tracker — the offer number, the offer date, the
  document link and the costing total — and the matching enquiry is updated
  automatically. The offer can also be filed into that enquiry's existing
  "Costing & Offer" drive folder.

== What is shared

[cols="1,1", options="header"]
|===
| Sent to the generator (pre-fill) | Returned to the enquiry (write-back)

| Company, contact, mobile, email | Offer number
| Item / equipment description | Offer date
| Sales & technical owners | Document link (Word / PDF)
| RFQ reference | Costing total
|===

The RFQ reference ties the two together: the generator always knows which
enquiry an offer belongs to, and the tracker always shows the latest offer
against each enquiry.

== Status & next steps

This integration is *documented but not yet built*. The agreed approach is to
keep both apps separate and connect them through a link and a small interface.
Building it would mean adding the "Generate Offer" link on the tracker, having
the generator accept the pre-filled details, and a small write-back step once
an offer is produced.
