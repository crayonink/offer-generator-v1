= RFQ Tracker Integration
:description: How the Offer Generator connects to the RFQ Enquiry Tracker.

_Status: proposed — documented, not yet built._

== Summary

ENCON runs two related tools. The *RFQ Enquiry Tracker* logs every incoming
request for quotation and follows it through to an offer. The *Offer Generator*
(this tool) produces the priced offer itself. At the moment the two are not
connected: the technical person generates the offer here and then uploads it
and the costing into the tracker by hand. This page sets out how to join the
two so that an offer is started from an enquiry (with the customer details
already filled in) and its results return to the enquiry automatically —
removing the manual upload and the re-keying.

== The two systems

*RFQ Enquiry Tracker.* A shared web app the team uses to log and follow every
enquiry. Each enquiry carries a reference (for example `ENC/RFQ/2026/001`), the
company and contact, the item, who it is assigned to, the sales and technical
owners, the target date, and the status. Once an offer exists, the enquiry also
holds the offer number, offer date, the document and the costing. Every enquiry
has its own Google Drive folder ("Costing & Offer") where the finished
documents are kept.

*Offer Generator.* This tool. The estimator enters the process parameters for a
piece of equipment; the tool sizes it, selects the components, prices the bill
of materials, and produces the offer as Word and PDF together with a costing
workbook, filing them to Google Drive.

Both run as separate web apps and both already work with Google Drive, which is
what makes a clean, loose connection between them practical.

== How it works today (manual)

. An enquiry is logged in the tracker with the company, contact and item.
. The technical person opens the Offer Generator separately and re-keys the
  customer and the item.
. The offer is generated — Word, PDF and the costing sheet.
. The technical person then *uploads the offer and the costing into the tracker
  by hand*, against the right enquiry, and types in the offer number and date.

This works, but it means the same information is entered twice, the upload is a
manual step that can be missed or delayed, and the tracker is only as current
as the last time someone remembered to update it.

== Proposed integration

The two apps stay separate — each keeps its own database and its own
deployment — and are joined loosely at two points: a link out from the tracker,
and a small write-back interface into it. Nothing about either app's internal
working changes, which keeps the change low-risk and lets either app be updated
on its own.

The proposed flow has three steps:

. *Start from the enquiry.* On an enquiry in the tracker, a "Generate Offer"
  link opens the Offer Generator with that enquiry's details already filled
  in — company, contact, mobile, email, the item, and the sales and technical
  owners — along with the RFQ reference.
. *Produce the offer.* The estimator completes the technical inputs and
  generates the priced offer as normal: the Word and PDF documents and the
  costing sheet.
. *Write the result back.* As the offer is finished, the generator sends the
  key results back to the tracker — the offer number, the offer date, the
  document link and the costing total — and the matching enquiry is updated
  automatically. The documents are filed into that enquiry's existing
  "Costing & Offer" Drive folder. This replaces the manual upload and re-keying
  done today.

== Data exchanged

When the offer is started from an enquiry, these details are carried across so
the estimator does not re-key them:

[cols="1,3", options="header"]
|===
| From the enquiry | Used in the offer as

| Company | Customer company
| Contact person, mobile, email | Customer contact details
| Item / equipment description | The equipment being quoted
| Sales owner | Sales / marketing person on the offer
| Technical owner | Technical person on the offer
| RFQ reference | The link key (see below)
|===

When the offer is finished, these results return to the same enquiry:

[cols="1,3", options="header"]
|===
| Back to the enquiry | Value

| Offer number | The generator's offer / quote number
| Offer date | Date the offer was produced
| Document link | Location of the Word / PDF offer (Drive)
| Costing | The offer total
|===

== The link key

The *RFQ reference* (for example `ENC/RFQ/2026/001`) ties the two together. It
travels out with the "Generate Offer" link and comes back with the write-back,
so the generator always knows which enquiry an offer belongs to and the tracker
always updates the right one. The offer number can also be matched on, for
offers created the other way round.

== Documents & Google Drive

Both apps already use Google Drive, and each enquiry already has its own
"Costing & Offer" folder. In the integrated flow the generator files the
finished offer and costing straight into that enquiry's folder, and the
document link it writes back points there — so the tracker, the enquiry folder
and the offer all line up without anyone moving files by hand.

== Rules & edge cases

* *Offer with no enquiry.* The generator can still be used on its own (no RFQ
  reference); it simply skips the write-back. The integration is additive.
* *Re-issuing an offer.* If an offer is regenerated for the same enquiry, the
  write-back updates the existing offer details rather than creating a second
  entry, and a new revision is filed to the same folder.
* *Offer number.* If an enquiry does not yet have an offer number, the
  generator's number becomes the enquiry's offer number on write-back; if it
  already has one, that number is reused.
* *Access.* The write-back is a controlled interface between the two apps, so
  only the generator can update enquiry offer details — the rest of the tracker
  is unaffected.

== What changes for each role

* *Technical person / estimator* — no more re-keying the customer, and no more
  manually uploading the offer and costing into the tracker.
* *Sales / reviewer* — the tracker shows the latest offer number, date,
  document and costing against each enquiry as soon as the offer is produced.

== Build outline (when approved)

On the *tracker* side: add a "Generate Offer" link on the enquiry that opens the
generator with the enquiry details, and a small write-back interface that
accepts the offer results and updates the enquiry.

On the *generator* side: accept the pre-filled details when opened from an
enquiry, carry the RFQ reference through, and call the tracker's write-back once
the offer is produced (and file the documents to the enquiry's Drive folder).

== Status & next steps

This integration is *documented but not yet built*. The agreed approach is to
keep both apps separate and connect them through a link and a small interface,
as above. The next step is to approve the build and schedule the two small
pieces of work on each side.
